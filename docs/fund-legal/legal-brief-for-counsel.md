## What Polarity Lab Is

Polarity Lab, LLC is a Delaware LLC. I set it up as an independent research institution that operates as a lab-as-fund. The founding premise is that no existing institution, academic, commercial, or governmental, is actually measuring what designed environments suppress from their users, or proving that recovery is possible through differently configured environments. That's the gap the lab exists to fill.

The way we do that is by building measurement instruments and running the products that generate the data those instruments require. The research and the products aren't separable. You have to operate the environments to measure them. The four instruments we're building are: the Integrity Delta (clinical AI honesty), the Proximity Index (community discovery infrastructure), the Cognitive Gap Measure (music recommendation), and the Format Effectiveness Measure (media formats).

The lab incubates research projects, provides shared infrastructure (legal, IP, financial operations, network), and takes 15% of each project's revenue in exchange. The remaining 85% distributes to the project team and early backers once the project reaches a defined runway threshold.

Active projects: WAXFEED (music platform and cognitive profiling instrument), Polarity GPS (community discovery platform and Proximity Index instrument), Integrity Delta (clinical AI honesty measurement framework), A Very Distant Perspective or AVDP (documentary series and format effectiveness study).

The lab has a website at polarity-lab.com and is beginning to accept early backer contributions.

## The Two-Track Contribution Model

We've structured contributions into two legally distinct tracks based on what is offered in exchange.

### Track 1: Under $1,000 (Gift / Perk model)

Contributions under $1,000 are gifts in exchange for perks. No financial return is promised or implied.

| Tier | What they receive |
|---|---|
| $10 to $99 | Founding credit on the project page |
| $100 to $999 | Founding credit + a physical gift (custom vinyl, signed artifact, or early product access depending on project) |

This is structurally the same as how Kickstarter campaigns have operated since 2009. The site only shows these tiers publicly. My read is that this falls outside SEC registration requirements.

**Question 1:** Is that read correct, that gift-for-perks contributions under $1,000 with no financial return are outside SEC registration requirements? The entity is a Delaware LLC with no fixed office. The site is national and online-only, so backers could be in any US state. Are there state-level blue sky laws that complicate this, and if so, which states should we watch?

**Question 2:** Do we need any specific disclosure language on our website beyond what we currently have ("$10-$999 contributions are gifts in exchange for perks. No financial return. Full terms at polarity-lab.com/legal")? What's the minimum we actually need to say?

### Track 2: $1,000 and above (Revenue Participation, Private Agreement)

Contributions of $1,000 or more participate in the project's financial believer pool, which receives approximately 15% of the project's 85% share once disbursements begin.

This tier is **not publicly advertised**. The website only shows "Founding Partner — get in touch with the team directly" language without specifying financial terms. Financial terms are discussed only in private conversations initiated by the prospective backer.

The intended structure:
- No general solicitation
- Relationship-based outreach only
- Up to 35 non-accredited investors, unlimited accredited investors
- Direct signed revenue participation agreement (no crowdfunding portal)
- Payment via ACH, check, or individual Stripe payment link
- Intended to fall under **Reg D Rule 506(b)**

**Revenue participation terms:**
- Pro-rata share of the financial believer pool
- Year 1 window from first disbursement date (not from investment date)
- No equity or ownership granted
- No decision-making authority over the lab or any project
- Rights expire after year 1. Full ownership reverts to the team.
- The lab can close the window and begin a traditional funding round at any time.
- Loss disclosure: if the project does not reach its runway threshold, contributions are a loss.

**Question 3:** Does this structure qualify for Reg D Rule 506(b) exemption? Specifically: does the "get in touch" language on the public website constitute general solicitation, even without specifying financial terms?

**Question 4:** What does the minimum viable revenue participation agreement look like? Do you have a template, or can you draft one? The key terms we need covered: pro-rata share of the financial believer pool, year 1 window from first disbursement date (not investment date), no equity or ownership granted, no decision-making authority, loss disclosure, IP stays with the lab, rights expire after year 1.

**Question 5:** Are there circumstances under which we'd need to register with FINRA or hold a broker-dealer license given this structure? I want to be certain before we accept any $1,000+ contributions.

## IP Structure

All projects agree at the start that IP transfers to Polarity Lab LLC. This is non-negotiable and gets settled before any work begins. The methodology, instruments, and any research outputs produced under the lab's infrastructure belong to the lab regardless of which collaborators contributed to their development.

Domain leaders who exit before disbursements begin forfeit all rights. Domain leaders who exit after disbursements begin retain what they've already been paid, but the IP stays with the lab.

## Compensation Structure

**Lab-wide core pool (from the lab's 15% cut):**

Lab-level roles, Founder/Executive Director, Director of Strategy and Partnerships, Lab Manager, Legal, HR, Sales, are compensated through a points-based core pool. Points are earned each quarter based on activity and reset quarterly. Each person's share of the core pool is proportional to their points. No fixed salaries until the lab can sustain them.

**Project collaborator compensation (from the project's 85% share):**

Once a project reaches its runway threshold, distributions flow to three pools: domain leader (approximately 40% of total revenue), time collaborators (approximately 30%), financial believers (approximately 15%). Collaborator weights within the time collaborator pool are determined by tier: core collaborator (1.0x), contributing collaborator (0.6x), advisor (0.25x).

**Question 9:** Are lab-wide roles employees, independent contractors, or something else under this structure? The absence of fixed compensation and the points-based variable pay model has employment classification implications I want to understand before anyone earns money.

## Context on the Projects

**Integrity Delta (IΔ):** A clinical AI honesty measurement framework. It measures the signed gap between what a model's internal representations indicate and what its final output actually states, specifically the degree to which an AI system suppresses its own correct reasoning under social or adversarial pressure. Pilot complete: n=5, Llama 3.1 8B, five clinical cases showing intermediate layers correctly identifying pathology while final outputs aligned with adversarial (incorrect) labels. Validation study at scale is the next milestone. Primary institutional buyers: clinical AI vendors, hospital systems, FDA-facing compliance functions. Gift tier: early access to the IΔ product.

**WAXFEED:** A music discovery platform that builds a cognitive profile from listening behavior, measuring the gap between algorithmically predicted preference and genuine taste response. Technical prototype in development. Primary buyers: consumers (subscription), research institutions (behavioral data licensing). Gift tier: custom vinyl record (production via Untitled.app).

**Polarity GPS:** A narrative location-based discovery platform that measures the gap between what a community produces and what discovery infrastructure actually surfaces to that community (the Proximity Index). Providence, RI is the first deployment city. Platform in development. Primary buyers: consumers (subscription), urban planning and city government institutions (Proximity Index assessments), DSA-facing platform audit functions. Gift tier: early access.

**A Very Distant Perspective (AVDP):** A documentary series studying format effectiveness, measuring what specific format conditions restore cognitive and emotional capacity that engagement media depletes. Existing footage, editorial in progress. Research affiliation in progress. Gift tier: signed production stills.

# Polarity Lab: Fund and Disbursement Model

**Status:** Internal working document. Not for publication. Needs legal review before public commitments are made.

**One-liner:** "Back the work before it's obvious. If the team gets paid, you do too."

## What You Are Backing

Every designed environment has a second threshold. The point where it stops extending human capacity and starts suppressing it. Schools that credential instead of teach. Clinical AI that overrides the correct diagnosis it just made. Music platforms that narrow genuine taste into predicted preference. Discovery infrastructure that makes a city invisible to itself.

This has been named. It has been documented. For fifty years the field has been stuck at the diagnosis. Nobody has closed the loop to recovery, because closing it requires operating the environments being measured, and operating them honestly requires independence from their commercial incentives. That is a structural position no existing institution occupies.

Polarity Lab occupies it.

The lab was founded on a proof of recovery. A founder with ADHD who watched every environment he entered, medicine, creative production, tech, make a trade with him. Taking something real in exchange for something measurable. He went back for the original version of himself. He built the first counter-environment (Cosmos). The capacity came back without altering who he was. That proof was one person. Now it is a methodology.

The lab's work is to perform that proof in every designed environment it enters: music, clinical AI, urban discovery, media. The instruments measure the gap between what the environment suppresses and what the underlying capacity actually is. The counter-environments close the gap. The before-and-after proves the return was real. Environment by environment, until recovery without suppression becomes the standard everything is designed to.

That is what you are backing. Not a product. Not a startup. The institution that holds the methodology and propagates it, building the proof that what designed environments suppress can be recovered, in every domain where the second threshold has been crossed.

## Governance Structure

Polarity Lab LLC is a single-member LLC. The founder is the sole member. This is deliberate.

The lab's methodology depends on independence. From funders, from collaborators who don't yet fully understand the vision, from any governance structure that could redirect the research agenda toward something other than what the lab was built to do. A multi-member ownership structure introduces exactly that risk. The single-member structure prevents it at the legal level, not just the philosophical one.

Collaborators, domain leads, core collaborators, advisors, directors, participate in the lab's economics through the revenue structure described in this document. They hold titles that reflect their function and contribution. They do not hold ownership or governance rights over the lab's direction. The algorithm lives in the founder. The LLC structure reflects that.

This does not mean the lab is closed. It means the vision is protected while it propagates. As collaborators deepen their understanding of the methodology and earn their way into it, roles can evolve. But governance transfer, if it ever happens, happens deliberately. Not by default and not ahead of understanding.

## Why the Lab-as-Fund Structure Exists

The lab's independence is not a nice-to-have. It is the asset. The instruments (Integrity Delta, Proximity Index, Cognitive Gap Measure, Format Effectiveness Measure) are only worth something because no funder can tell the lab what to find. The moment that independence is in question, the methodology loses its value. Not gradually. Immediately.

Most financial structures create dependencies that compromise this. Equity investors need growth and exits that push product prioritization over research integrity. Grants can be captured by funder agendas. Revenue from a single dominant product creates pressure to protect that product's findings.

The lab-as-fund model was designed to prevent all three. The 15% lab cut arrives immediately off every dollar, before any project distribution, so the lab's operations are never hostage to any single product's success or failure. Revenue participation rights are time-bounded, not permanent equity, so no investor accumulates a position they can use as leverage over the research agenda. Time outranks money in the distribution structure so the team doing the work, whose judgment the methodology depends on, is never overruled by financial interests.

The structure protects the thing the structure is built to serve.

## Two Distinct Investment Tracks

Early believer participation splits into two legally distinct products at the $1,000 threshold.

### Track 1: Under $1,000 (Gift / Patron model)

Contributions under $1,000 are gifts in exchange for perks. No financial return. No expectation of one. Structurally identical to Kickstarter, which has operated since 2009 under the same model with no SEC registration required. The key is that no profit participation is promised at these tiers.

| Amount | What they get |
|---|---|
| $10 to $99 | Founding credit on the project page and site |
| $100 to $999 | Founding credit + a physical gift from the project (custom vinyl, branded artifact relevant to the work) |

These tiers build the community of genuine believers before the work is obvious. They are also the lab's public-facing funding surface. The $1,000+ tier is not publicly advertised.

### Track 2: $5,000+ (Revenue Participation, Private Agreement)

Contributions of $5,000 or more participate in the financial believer disbursement pool. This tier is offered privately to people who reach out and have a conversation with the team. It is not publicly advertised on the site.

This structure is designed to fall under Reg D Rule 506(b): no general solicitation, relationship-based, up to 35 non-accredited investors plus unlimited accredited investors. No crowdfunding portal required. Legal review needed to confirm this read before any agreements are signed.

**When a project's financial believer pool is open**

The pool is not open by default. It opens based on where the project is:

| Project state | Financial believer pool |
|---|---|
| Pre-revenue, no signed institutional commitment | Closed. Gift tier only. |
| Signed institutional commitment or within 60 days of first revenue | Open. Capped at raise formula. |
| Runway threshold hit, active disbursements | Pool can expand for next raise cycle. |

This means financial belief is sold against demonstrated progress, not a cold promise. WAXFEED and GPS do not open financial believer pools until they have a signed institutional commitment or clear revenue in sight. IΔ can open its pool once the first proof contract is signed.

**The raise cap formula**

The lab sets a maximum total raise from believers per project before any agreements are executed. The cap targets a 2x minimum return for believers if the project hits its expected year-1 revenue:

```
Raise cap = Expected Year-1 Revenue × 0.077
```

**How to calculate expected year-1 revenue**

The expected revenue figure is a bottom-up estimate, not an aspiration. It is built differently depending on the project type and set conservatively.

For institutional revenue projects (IΔ):

```
Expected Revenue = (Signed contracts, full value)
                 + (Late-stage pipeline × 60% close probability)
                 + (Early pipeline × 20% close probability)
```

No signed contracts means no open financial believer pool, regardless of pipeline confidence. The trigger for opening the pool is a signed commitment, not a projection.

For consumer subscription projects (WAXFEED, GPS):

```
Expected Revenue = Projected active subscribers × monthly price
                   × weighted months active in year 1
```

Use conservative subscriber ramp assumptions. Launch month matters — a product launching in month 6 has six months of ramp, not twelve. Until the product is live and generating behavioral data, subscriber projections are speculative and the raise cap will be too small to open a financial believer pool. That is the correct answer.

For the general fund:

```
General fund Expected Revenue = Sum of expected year-1 revenue
                                 across all projects currently in distributions
```

Only projects that have hit their runway threshold count. Projects still in ramp do not.

These calculations are documented in the project agreement at launch and disclosed to every financial believer before signing.

| Expected Year-1 Revenue | Raise cap (2x target) | Believers at $5k min | Believers at $10k min |
|---|---|---|---|
| $100k | $7,700 | 1 | 0 |
| $200k | $15,400 | 3 | 1 |
| $300k | $23,100 | 4 | 2 |
| $500k | $38,500 | 7 | 3 |
| $1M | $77,000 | 15 | 7 |

The expected revenue figure is set at project launch, documented in the project agreement, and shown to every believer before they sign. The raise cap is hard. Once it is reached, the pool closes until the next raise cycle.

**How it works operationally:**
1. Person reaches out via email or call request
2. Team has a conversation, explains the model, shows the math
3. If both parties want to proceed, a revenue participation agreement is signed directly
4. Payment collected via ACH, check, or individual Stripe payment link
5. Backer added to the project's believer pool with pro-rata share documented
6. When disbursements begin, the lab pushes payment to them directly

The conversation-to-invest path is a feature, not a friction. A $5,000+ decision warrants a relationship. The genuine believer who understands the work is a different thing from an anonymous transaction. That relationship is part of what makes early belief valuable to both sides.

**Wefunder / Republic as a later option:** If the lab moves to broad public campaigns at scale, these platforms become relevant. Until then, direct agreements keep costs lower (platforms take 6-8%) and relationship quality higher.

**What they receive:**
- Pro-rata share of the project's financial believer pool during year 1 of personnel disbursements
- Quarterly research updates from the team
- All perks from lower tiers
- A direct relationship with the team

**What they do not receive:**
- Equity or ownership in the lab or any project
- Decision-making authority over research agenda or findings
- Permanent revenue rights

**How they get paid:**
The lab calculates each believer's pro-rata share at each disbursement and pushes payment directly via ACH or equivalent. Payment details and disbursement mechanics are specified in the signed agreement.

**Break-even disclosure**

Every financial believer agreement includes this figure:

> "At the raise cap for this project ($X total raised), your investment returns flat if the project generates $Y in year-1 revenue. The project's expected year-1 revenue is $Z."

This is not optional language. It is in every agreement, regardless of how confident the team is in the revenue projection.

## Revenue Flow

### Step 1: Lab cut, immediately

Every dollar of revenue that arrives, the lab takes 15% off the top right away. The lab invested first, in the infrastructure, the methodology, the flywheel that makes each project possible. It gets paid first, every time.

### Step 2: Accumulate runway

The remaining 85% accumulates in the project account. No distributions to anyone until the account holds 3 to 6 months of operating expenses. The exact threshold is set at project launch and documented in the project agreement.

### Step 3: Distributions begin

Once the runway threshold is hit, the first personnel disbursements go out. This moment starts year 1 for financial believers: 12 rolling months from first disbursement, not from investment date. From this point, every new dollar of the 85% distributes immediately across the pools.

**If the project never hits its runway threshold:** Early believer contributions are a loss. Money has been spent building the project. The lab's responsibility is to select and develop projects that make it. This must be stated clearly in all materials shown to potential believers.

## Distribution Structure

### Layer 1: Lab Cut (15%, immediate)

Splits into two internal pools:

**Operations reserve:** Legal, IP filing, patenting, infrastructure, network costs. This is what makes the flywheel run.

**Core pool:** Lab-wide roles, Executive Director, Director of Strategy and Partnerships, Lab Manager, Legal, Sales, HR. Each earns from the core pool proportionally by engagement points earned in the quarter. No fixed salary until the lab can sustain it. Points reset each quarter so compensation always reflects current contribution.

### Core Pool Points System

All core pool roles earn points through a unified engagement currency. Points are not dollars. They are units of contribution. At disbursement, each person's share equals their points divided by total points earned across all roles that quarter.

**Base points per active month:**

| Role | Base pts/month |
|---|---|
| Executive Director | 40 |
| Director of Strategy and Partnerships | 20 |
| Lab Manager | 20 |
| Legal | 15 |
| HR | 15 |
| Sales | 10 |

**Activity bonuses:**

| Role | Activity | Bonus pts |
|---|---|---|
| Director of Strategy | Partnership or affiliation closed | +5 |
| Sales | Per $1,000 raised (gift tier) | +2 |
| Sales | Per $1,000 raised ($1,000+ tier) | +3 |
| Legal | Agreement executed | +10 |
| Legal | Patent filed | +15 |
| HR | Collaborator onboarded | +5 |
| Lab Manager | Quarterly project review completed | +5 |
| Executive Director | Project milestone hit (per project) | +varies |

Sales earns more points per dollar at the higher tier because those agreements require more relationship work. But points always convert to a fraction of the core pool. If the lab earns less, everyone earns less. Incentives are fully aligned.

**Quarterly reset:** Points do not carry forward. Each quarter is a fresh accounting of actual contribution. Early members cannot coast on historical accumulation while current high-contributors are undercompensated.

**Example (one quarter, core pool = $8,000):**

| Role | Points earned | Share | Payout |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Director | 140 | 33% | $2,640 |
| Director of Strategy | 75 | 18% | $1,440 |
| Sales | 95 | 22% | $1,760 |
| Lab Manager | 65 | 15% | $1,200 |
| Legal | 55 | 13% | $1,040 |
| **Total** | **430** | **100%** | **$8,080*** |

*Rounding adjusted.*

### Layer 2: Project Distribution (85%)

| Pool | Default % of 85% | Approximate % of total revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Domain leader | ~47% | ~40% |
| Time collaborator pool | ~35% | ~30% |
| Financial believer pool | ~18% | ~15% |

All splits are agreed and signed before disbursements begin. Each project is a separate agreement.

## Contributor Tiers

Tiers are defined at project start, locked in at acceptance, and determine weight within the time collaborator pool.

| Tier | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Core collaborator | 1.0x | Daily involvement, load-bearing work |
| Contributing collaborator | 0.6x | Regular involvement, specific domain |
| Advisor | 0.25x | Periodic, specialized input |

**Vesting and performance review:** Collaborator weights vest on a quarterly schedule. The Executive Director conducts quarterly reviews for all active projects. Weight can be upgraded by agreement. Consistent non-performance results in weight reduction or exit, documented and agreed in the original project agreement. This clause must be written into every project agreement at start.

## Domain Leader Exit Policy

**Exit before profit disbursements begin:**
Forfeits pool. Lab retains all IP. Remaining team or lab determines whether project continues.

**Exit after profit disbursements begin:**
Keeps what has already been paid. Must name a successor for the project to continue under the lab model. Lab retains IP regardless.

IP transfers to the lab at project start. This is non-negotiable. The methodology is the lab's core asset and cannot be fragmented across projects that exit.

**Project shutdown:**
If a project fails to reach its runway threshold and shuts down, it exits the lab, surrenders IP, and any remaining assets go to the lab. For believers this is always a risk. The general fund is the safer bet, whole portfolio exposure, lower individual upside, versus a specific project, which has higher upside and higher risk. The structural parallel: individual stocks versus the index.

## General Fund: The Lab as Portfolio

Backing the general fund is backing the lab itself as a project. Same mechanics as any project: 3-6 month runway threshold, year 1 window from first lab-level disbursements, loss if the lab fails before trigger.

Lower upside than a specific project. Exposure to the whole portfolio.

**General fund allocation within the 15% lab cut**

The lab's 15% cut splits three ways:

| Pool | Share of 15% | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Operations reserve | 50% | Legal, IP, infrastructure, network |
| Core pool | 25% | Lab-wide roles, points system |
| General fund believer pool | 25% | Pro-rata return to general fund believers |

These percentages are defaults. The lab can adjust the operations reserve and general fund believer split by resolution of the founder, but the core pool floor is 25% until the lab sustains fixed salaries.

**General fund raise cap**

The same raise cap formula applies, using aggregate lab-level revenue as the base:

```
General fund raise cap = Expected Year-1 Lab Revenue × 0.077
```

The general fund believer pool is 25% of the 15% lab cut, which is 3.75% of total lab revenue. At the 2x target, the raise cap is 3.75% / 2 = 1.875% of expected revenue. The 0.077 formula (which targets 2x on the project pool of 15.3%) does not apply to the general fund directly. The general fund formula is:

```
General fund raise cap = Expected Year-1 Aggregate Revenue × 0.019
```

Until the lab has active revenue from at least two projects, the general fund financial believer pool stays limited:
- Minimum: $10,000
- Maximum total raised: $30,000
- Maximum 3 believers

Once the lab has revenue from two or more projects, the minimum can come down and the cap can expand in proportion to aggregate revenue.

The general fund is the right entry for believers who are drawn to the methodology itself rather than any specific product. The people who understand that the lab's gold is the algorithm, not the application.

## The Infinite Equity Property

Revenue participation rights are time-bounded. They are not permanent equity.

- Project raises early believer funding before launch
- Year 1 window opens once runway is stable
- Rights expire after year 1. Full ownership reverts to the team.
- Next round starts fresh with no dilution

The team owns the methodology permanently. Early believers are rewarded for backing the work before it was obvious, which is exactly what they did. After year 1, the lab and its people own what they built.

The company can close the window and move to a traditional funding round at any time.

## The Already-Funded Project Advantage

The proof contract model (institutional buyers paying for IΔ assessments, Proximity Index audits) creates a category of projects with existing demand and a credible path to revenue before consumer scale is achieved. These are not moonshots. A project with a hospital system as an early assessment partner and an FDA regulatory tailwind has a fundamentally different risk profile than a cold consumer launch.

Leading with these de-risked institutional projects builds the lab's track record. That track record makes the earlier-stage consumer projects easier to pitch to believers. This is the flywheel on the fundraising side.

The current grant and institutional funding climate is difficult for independent researchers. This model gives them an alternative channel that is faster, more direct, and aligned with genuine believers in the work rather than institutional gatekeepers.

## Sales and Outreach

The lab's sales and partnerships team are lab members compensated through the core pool points system. Not commissioned salespeople. That distinction matters legally.

For the gift tier (under $1,000): team members describe the work and direct people to the project page. No securities activity.

For the $1,000+ tier: team members have conversations with interested parties and explain the model. Because the $1,000+ option is not publicly advertised and transactions are direct private agreements, this is relationship outreach, not regulated securities solicitation. Legal review needed to confirm this holds in all relevant jurisdictions.

The points system means sales is rewarded for results but always as a fraction of the lab's own revenue. If the lab earns less, everyone earns less. No fixed commission rate. No separate compensation tied specifically to securities transactions.

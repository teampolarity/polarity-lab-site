# Quarterly Review — Process and Template

**Polarity Lab**
**Owner:** Lab Manager (runs the review, documents outcomes)
**Decision authority:** Executive Director (all weight adjustments and exits)
**Frequency:** Once per quarter, within 14 days of quarter end
**Bonus points:** Lab Manager earns +5 pts per completed review

---

## Overview

Every active project gets a quarterly review. Every active collaborator on every project gets assessed. The review produces a written record that: (a) documents collaborator contribution against tier expectations, (b) records any weight adjustments, and (c) surfaces anything requiring the founder's attention or decision.

The quarterly review is also the accountability mechanism for domain leads. The instrument and the research agenda are theirs. Whether they are moving them is what the review asks.

There are two review types:
- **Project review** — covers a project's milestone progress, runway status, and all collaborators on that project
- **Core pool role review** — covers lab-wide roles (Legal, HR, Sales, Lab Manager, Director of Strategy)

Run both in the same quarterly cycle.

## Process Notes

**Who runs the review:** Lab Manager schedules and facilitates. Domain lead attends the project review for their project. Executive Director receives the summary and makes all weight adjustment decisions.

**How long it takes:** 30-45 minutes per project review when the domain lead is prepared. 15 minutes for core pool review. The quarterly summary is a synthesis of what was documented in the project reviews.

**Where documents live:** Completed review documents go in the lab's operational records. The quarterly summary goes to the founder directly.

**What happens after:** Founder approves or modifies any recommended weight adjustments within 7 days of receiving the summary. Lab Manager notifies affected collaborators in writing within 14 days of quarter end (per the collaborator agreement).

**If a collaborator has two consecutive Minimal or Absent quarters:** Lab Manager flags for exit conversation. Founder decides. Exit terms follow the collaborator agreement.

**If a domain lead shows a research integrity concern:** This goes directly to the founder, not into the normal review process. The instrument is what keeps the project honest. A domain lead drifting their project toward product without evidence is the highest-risk failure mode in the lab.

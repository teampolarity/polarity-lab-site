## 1. Background

The Lab is an independent research institution building measurement instruments and counter-environments across multiple domains, including clinical AI honesty, music recommendation, community discovery, media formats, and personal operating systems. The Lab operates on a lab-as-fund model: each project accumulates revenue, retains a runway reserve, and distributes the remainder to the project team and early backers once the project reaches a defined threshold.

This Agreement governs Backer's participation in the financial believer pool for the **[PROJECT NAME]** project ("Project").

## 3. Revenue Participation

**3.1 Financial believer pool.** Upon the Project reaching its runway threshold (as described in Section 4), Backer is entitled to a pro-rata share of the Project's financial believer pool.

**3.2 Pool definition.** The financial believer pool receives approximately 15% of the Project's 85% revenue share, which equals approximately 12.75% of total Project revenue. [NOTE: This is approximately. Exact percentage is documented in the Project agreement at launch.]

**3.3 Pro-rata share.** Backer's share of the financial believer pool equals:

```
Backer share = Backer Contribution / Total raised from all financial believers
```

The total raised from financial believers for the Project is capped at the raise cap defined in Section 5. Backer's pro-rata share will not be diluted below what this formula produces at the time of execution.

**3.4 Year-1 window.** The financial believer pool distributes during year 1 only. Year 1 is defined as the 12 rolling months beginning on the date of the Project's first personnel disbursement ("First Disbursement Date"). Year 1 does not begin on the date of this Agreement or the date of Backer's Contribution. It begins when the Project starts paying its team.

**3.5 Distribution frequency.** Disbursements from the financial believer pool are made on the same schedule as project team distributions, no less than quarterly. The Lab will push payment to Backer via [ACH / check] at each distribution.

**3.6 Expiration.** Rights under this Agreement expire at the end of year 1 (12 months from First Disbursement Date). After expiration, the Lab and its team retain full ownership of all Project revenue. No further distributions are owed to Backer.

## 4. Runway Threshold

**4.1 Definition.** The Project's runway threshold is defined as the point at which the Project's accumulated revenue reserve equals [3 / 6] months of Project operating expenses, as documented in the Project agreement.

**4.2 Pre-threshold.** Until the runway threshold is reached, no disbursements are made to any party, including the project team or financial believers.

**4.3 No guarantee.** The Lab does not guarantee that the Project will reach its runway threshold. If it does not, Backer's Contribution is a loss. See Section 7 (Loss Disclosure).

## 6. Break-Even Disclosure

> At the raise cap for this Project ($[RAISE CAP] total raised), Backer's Contribution returns flat if the Project generates **$[BREAK-EVEN REVENUE]** in year-1 revenue. The Project's expected year-1 revenue is $[EXPECTED REVENUE].

Break-even revenue is calculated as:

```
Break-even = Raise cap / (Financial believer pool % × 85%)
           = [RAISE CAP] / 0.1275
```

This disclosure is not a projection or guarantee. It is the mathematical threshold for a flat return, presented so Backer can assess the risk clearly.

## 7. Loss Disclosure

**Backer acknowledges and accepts the following:**

- If the Project does not reach its runway threshold, Backer's Contribution is a total loss. The Lab will have spent the contributed funds on building the Project.
- There is no guarantee of any return on the Contribution.
- Revenue participation rights expire after year 1 regardless of the Project's performance in subsequent years.
- The Lab may close the financial believer pool and pursue traditional funding at any time, in which case no new contributions will be accepted. Existing believers' rights under signed agreements are unaffected.
- Past performance of other Lab projects, if any, does not predict the performance of this Project.

## 8. What Backer Does Not Receive

Backer's Contribution does not entitle Backer to:

- Equity, ownership, or membership interest in the Lab or the Project
- Decision-making authority over the Lab's research agenda, project direction, or financial decisions
- Access to the Lab's internal financial records beyond the quarterly research updates described in Section 9
- Any right to direct how the Contribution is spent
- Any right to a refund, except as required by applicable law
- Permanent revenue rights of any kind

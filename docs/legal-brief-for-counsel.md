# Polarity Lab: Legal Brief for Counsel

**Status:** For attorney review. Do not distribute externally.
**Prepared:** April 2026
**Sender:** Polarity Lab leadership
**Entity:** Polarity Lab, LLC, formed in Delaware
**Geography:** No fixed office or HQ. Loosely based in Providence, RI currently but fully remote and online. No geographic restriction on backers or collaborators. US-focused for now.

---

## What Polarity Lab Is

Polarity Lab, LLC is a Delaware LLC operating as an independent research lab and fund. It incubates research projects, provides shared infrastructure (legal, IP, financial operations, network), and takes 15% of each project's revenue in exchange. The remaining 85% distributes to the project team and early backers once the project reaches a defined runway threshold.

The lab currently has four research fields: Film Therapeutics, AI Honesty, Cognitive Profiling, and Community Discovery. Each field has one or more projects in development. Active projects: A Very Distant Perspective (AVDP), WAXFEED, Integrity Delta, PolarityGPS.

The lab has a website at polarity-lab.com and is beginning to accept early backer contributions.

---

## The Two-Track Contribution Model

We have structured contributions into two legally distinct tracks based on what is offered in exchange.

### Track 1: Under $1,000 (Gift / Perk model)

Contributions under $1,000 are gifts in exchange for perks. No financial return is promised or implied.

| Tier | What they receive |
|---|---|
| $10 to $99 | Founding credit on the project page |
| $100 to $999 | Founding credit + a physical gift (custom vinyl, signed artifact, or early product access, depending on project) |

This is structurally identical to how Kickstarter campaigns have operated since 2009. The site only shows these tiers publicly. We believe this falls outside SEC registration requirements.

**Question 1:** Is our read correct that gift-for-perks contributions under $1,000 with no financial return are outside SEC registration requirements? The entity is a Delaware LLC with no fixed office. The site is national and online-only, so backers could be in any US state. Are there any state-level blue sky laws that complicate this, and if so, which states should we watch?

**Question 2:** Do we need any specific disclosure language on our website beyond what we currently have ("$10-$999 contributions are gifts in exchange for perks. No financial return. Full terms at polarity-lab.com/legal")? What is the minimum sufficient disclosure?

---

### Track 2: $1,000 and above (Revenue Participation, Private Agreement)

Contributions of $1,000 or more participate in the project's financial believer pool, which receives approximately 15% of the project's 85% share once disbursements begin.

This tier is **not publicly advertised**. The website only shows "Founding Partner - get in touch with the team directly" language without specifying financial terms. Financial terms are discussed only in private conversations initiated by the prospective backer.

The intended structure:
- No general solicitation
- Relationship-based outreach only
- Up to 35 non-accredited investors, unlimited accredited investors
- Direct signed revenue participation agreement (no crowdfunding portal)
- Payment via ACH, check, or individual Stripe payment link
- This is intended to fall under **Reg D Rule 506(b)**

**Question 3:** Does this structure qualify for Reg D Rule 506(b) exemption? Specifically: does the "get in touch" language on the public website constitute general solicitation, even without specifying financial terms?

**Question 4:** What is the minimum viable revenue participation agreement we need? Do you have a template or can you draft one? The key terms we need covered: pro-rata share of the financial believer pool, year 1 window from first disbursement date (not investment date), no equity or ownership granted, no decision-making authority, loss disclosure, IP stays with the lab.

**Question 5:** Are there circumstances under which we would need to register with FINRA or hold a broker-dealer license given this structure? We want to be certain before we accept any $1,000+ contributions.

## IP Structure

All projects in the lab agree at the start that IP transfers to Polarity Lab. This is non-negotiable and set before any work begins. Domain leaders who exit before disbursements begin forfeit all rights. Domain leaders who exit after disbursements begin retain what they have been paid but the IP stays with the lab.

**Question 6:** What should the IP transfer language look like in a project agreement? We want it airtight but fair. Does IP transfer at execution of the agreement, or does it accrue as work is produced?

**Question 7:** For projects with existing institutional affiliations (e.g., university-affiliated researchers, grant-funded work), does the IP transfer language create any conflict with their institution's IP policies? What should we watch for?

---

## Compensation Structure

Lab-wide roles (Executive Director, Strategy, Lab Manager, Legal, HR, Sales) are compensated through a "core pool" that comes from the lab's 15% cut. They earn points each quarter based on activity; their share of the core pool is proportional to their points. Points reset each quarter. There are no fixed salaries until the lab can sustain them.

Project collaborators (domain leaders, core collaborators, contributing collaborators, advisors) are compensated from the project's 85% share once disbursements begin.

**Question 8:** Are lab-wide roles employees, independent contractors, or something else under this structure? The absence of fixed compensation and the points-based variable pay model has employment classification implications we want to understand before anyone earns money.

**Question 9:** Sales team members who discuss the $1,000+ option with prospective backers are compensated through the core pool points system, not on commission. Does this structure remove the broker-dealer registration obligation for those individuals? What is the line we need to stay on the right side of?

---

## General Fund

The lab also accepts contributions to a "General Fund" that backs the lab as a whole rather than a specific project. Same two-track structure: gifts under $1,000, revenue participation above $1,000. The general fund's financial believer pool receives a share of the lab's aggregate 15% cut once the lab itself hits its runway threshold.

**Question 10:** Does the general fund create any additional regulatory complexity versus per-project contributions? Is it treated the same way under Reg D?

---

## Loss Disclosure

We are committed to clear loss disclosure. The current language on our website: "If a project does not reach its runway threshold, early contributions are a loss. There is no guarantee of return. Back the work because you believe in it."

**Question 11:** Is this language sufficient? What else do we need to say, and where must it appear (website, signed agreement, elsewhere)?

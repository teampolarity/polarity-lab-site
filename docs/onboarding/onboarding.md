## Welcome

You were not hired. You were recognized.

The people who work at Polarity Lab were brought in because they have lived some version of the phenomenon the lab exists to address. Before you understood the lab's thesis intellectually, you had already encountered it personally — in an institution, a system, an environment that made a trade with you and kept running as if nothing happened. That experience is not incidental to your role here. It is the qualification.

This document will orient you to what the lab is, how it works, what you are agreeing to by being here, and what you will never be asked to compromise. Read it alongside the philosophy document. They work together.

## What You Have Joined

Every designed environment has a second threshold. Below it, the environment delivers on what it promises. It extends the capacity it was built to serve. Beyond it, something changes. The optimization for the environment's own metrics diverges so far from the underlying human capacity those metrics were meant to represent that the environment begins to suppress the thing it was designed to develop.

Schools that produce credentials instead of learning. Clinical AI that produces agreeable outputs instead of honest ones. Music platforms that produce predicted preferences instead of genuine taste. Discovery infrastructure that makes a community invisible to itself. Productivity systems that offer to help by replacing who you are with something more processable.

Ivan Illich named this in 1973. He called it counterproductivity. He could not measure it. The field after him spent fifty years describing instances of it. Nobody closed the loop to recovery — nobody built the counter-environment, ran the instruments, and proved the suppressed capacity actually comes back.

Polarity Lab closes the loop.

The lab was founded on a proof of recovery. The founder built the first counter-environment for his own suppressed capacity — ADHD, executive dysfunction, the cognitive self that every optimized system tried to trade away. The capacity came back without altering who he was. That proof was one person. Now it is a methodology. The lab's work is to perform that proof in every environment that has crossed the second threshold, until recovery without suppression becomes the standard everything is designed to.

That is what you are here to build.

## Why You Are Here

The principle is not flexible: the domain lead and the people closest to the work must have lived some version of the second threshold before they run the project.

You cannot design the counter-environment from outside the experience of needing one. The pattern recognition that tells you where the suppression is, what recovery actually feels like versus what just registers as improvement on a metric, how to distinguish a counter-environment from a differently configured suppression — that comes from having been the subject, not from studying subjects.

This does not mean your experience has to match the founder's. The second threshold shows up everywhere. You may have lived it in an institution, in a social system, in an identity, in a creative environment, in a technical system. What matters is that you know the mechanism from inside.

If you are uncertain whether this describes you, it probably does. People who have not experienced the second threshold do not usually recognize what the lab is describing. The fact that you are here suggests you do.

## How the Work Works

**The product is the instrument. The instrument is the research. They are the same thing.**

This is not metaphor. You cannot measure what WAXFEED's recommendation environment suppresses without running WAXFEED. You cannot validate the Integrity Delta at clinical scale without deploying clinical AI in controlled settings. The research requires the environment. The environment requires the product. The product requires users. This is why the lab operates products rather than just studying them.

What this means for how you work: every decision about the product is also a decision about the research. A feature that grows users at the expense of instrument integrity is not a neutral tradeoff. It breaks the thing the product is for. When those two things appear to be in tension, the instrument integrity wins. Always.

**The return has to be real before it gets called a return.**

The lab's entire credibility rests on this. If you are working toward a milestone that will be called a proof of recovery, it has to be measurable, honest about what it did and did not restore, and not inflated to satisfy a deadline or a funder. A partial return published as a complete one destroys the value of every instrument the lab has built and will build.

**The demonstration is the argument.**

You do not persuade people that recovery is possible by explaining the methodology. You show them. The proof of concept is the pitch. The case study is the marketing. The working product is the evidence. If the return is real, it speaks. If it is not real, no argument makes it so.

## The Economic Structure

Polarity Lab operates as a lab-as-fund. Every project distributes profits through a layered structure:

**Layer 1:** 15% off every dollar of revenue goes to the lab immediately. The lab invested first in the infrastructure, methodology, and flywheel that makes your project possible. It gets paid first, every time.

**Layer 2:** The remaining 85% accumulates in the project account until it holds 3 to 6 months of operating expenses (the runway threshold, set at project start). Once that threshold is hit, distributions begin. From that point, every new dollar distributes immediately.

**The 85% splits across three pools:**
- Domain leader pool: approximately 40% of total revenue
- Time collaborator pool: approximately 30% of total revenue
- Financial believer pool: approximately 15% of total revenue

**Your weight within the time collaborator pool depends on your tier:**
- Core collaborator (1.0x): daily involvement, load-bearing work
- Contributing collaborator (0.6x): regular involvement, specific domain
- Advisor (0.25x): periodic, specialized input

Your tier was agreed and documented when you joined. It is reviewed quarterly. Strong performance can move it up. Consistent non-performance moves it down or ends the relationship. The review is honest and documented in advance.

**If the project does not reach its runway threshold:** the financial believers absorb the loss. The team absorbs the time. There is no guarantee of compensation before the threshold is hit. This is the reality of early-stage work and it is stated plainly rather than obscured.

The full mechanics — including the core pool points system for lab-level roles, the financial believer model for external investors, the exit policies, and the general fund — are documented in full in the fund model document.

## The Governance Structure

Polarity Lab LLC is a single-member LLC. The founder is the sole member.

This means: you participate in the lab's economics through the revenue structure above. You hold a title that reflects your function and contribution. You do not hold governance rights over the lab's research agenda, methodology, or direction.

This is not a hierarchy built on ego. It is a structure built on the same logic as everything else in the lab. The algorithm — the accumulated pattern recognition that makes the methodology work — lives in one person. The vision requires protection from dilution by people who are still developing their understanding of it. The structure enforces that protection at the legal level.

As your understanding of the full vision deepens, your role can evolve. That evolution is earned, not assumed. The founder is paying attention.

## What the Lab Will Not Do

These are not guidelines. They are the edges of what the lab is. Work that requires crossing them is not Polarity Lab work.

The instrument does not change its finding because of who paid for the assessment. If an institutional buyer's product is suppressing capacity, that finding gets published. Independence is the asset. The moment a finding becomes unpublishable because of a funder relationship, the methodology is worthless.

The counter-environment restores without altering. What the person, community, or system gets back has to be the thing that was there before — not a reshaped version that is more legible to the environment but unrecognizable to the person it was restored to. Optimization that makes the return measurable but inauthentic is a different suppression.

The lab enters an environment because the question is genuine, not because the market is large. If the only argument for a project is market size, it is not a Polarity Lab project.

No funder sets the research agenda. No growth target overrides instrument integrity. No urgency makes a partial return into a complete one.

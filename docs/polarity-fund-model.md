# Polarity Lab: Fund and Disbursement Model

**Status:** Internal working document. Not for publication. Needs legal review before public commitments are made.

**One-liner:** "Back the work before it's obvious. If the team gets paid, you do too."

---

## Core Principle

Polarity Lab operates as a lab-as-fund structured like an academic research lab. It incubates projects, builds shared infrastructure, and takes a cut of each project's output in exchange for the flywheel it provides: legal, IP, patenting, collaborative network, and compounding effort that gets projects completed faster and everyone paid sooner.

The lab itself is a project under this same model. Its revenue is the 15% it collects from every sub-project. It has a domain leader, time collaborators, and financial believers, and its own runway threshold. The structure is self-similar at every level.

---

## Organizational Structure

Polarity Lab maps directly onto an academic lab structure. This framing makes the model legible to researchers, institutions, and serious collaborators who already understand lab economics.

| Academic role | Polarity Lab equivalent | Disbursement tier |
|---|---|---|
| Principal Investigator | Executive Director | Core pool (lab-wide) |
| PhD / Domain Lead | Project Founder / Sub-lab Director | Domain leader pool |
| Postdoc | Core Collaborator | 1.0x weight |
| PhD Candidate | Contributing Collaborator | 0.6x weight |
| Advisor | Advisor | 0.25x weight |
| Grant / Institutional Funder | Early Believer ($1,000+) | Financial believer pool |
| Foundation gift / Patron | Early Believer ($10–$999) | Perks only, no financial return |

The Executive Director (PI) directs the lab's overall research agenda and oversees all projects. Domain leads run their projects as sub-labs with specific research interests. Collaborators contribute within projects. Funders participate in disbursements or receive perks depending on tier.

## Two Distinct Product Tracks

Early believer participation splits into two legally distinct products at the $1,000 threshold.

### Track 1: Under $1,000 (Gift / Patron model)

Contributions under $1,000 are gifts in exchange for perks. There is no financial return and no expectation of one. This is structurally identical to Kickstarter, which has operated since 2009 under the same model with no SEC registration required. The key is that no profit participation is promised at these tiers.

| Amount | What they get |
|---|---|
| $10 to $99 | Founding credit on the project page and site |
| $100 to $999 | Above + a physical gift from the project (custom vinyl, branded artifact relevant to the work) |

### Track 2: $1,000+ (Revenue Participation, Private Agreement)

Contributions of $1,000 or more participate in the financial believer disbursement pool. This tier is not publicly advertised on the site. Only the gift tiers are shown publicly. The $1,000+ option is offered privately to people who reach out and have a conversation with the team.

This structure is designed to fall under Reg D Rule 506(b): no general solicitation, relationship-based, up to 35 non-accredited investors plus unlimited accredited investors. No crowdfunding portal required. Legal review needed to confirm this read before any agreements are signed.

**How it works operationally:**
1. Person reaches out via email or call request
2. Team has a conversation, explains the model, answers questions
3. If both parties want to proceed, a revenue participation agreement is signed directly
4. Payment collected (ACH, check, or Stripe payment link sent to that individual)
5. Backer added to the project's believer pool with their pro-rata share documented
6. When disbursements begin, the lab pushes payment to them directly

The email-to-invest path is actually a better product at this tier. A $1,000+ decision warrants a conversation. The relationship built in that conversation is part of what makes someone a genuine early believer, not just a transaction.

**Wefunder / Republic as a later option:** If the lab moves to broad public campaigns at scale, these platforms become relevant. Until then, direct agreements keep costs lower (platforms take 6-8%) and the relationship higher quality.

**What they receive:**
- Pro-rata share of the project's financial believer pool during year 1 of personnel disbursements
- Quarterly research updates from the team
- All perks from lower tiers
- A direct relationship with the team

**What they do not receive:**
- Equity or ownership
- Decision-making authority
- Permanent revenue rights

**How they get paid:**
The lab calculates each believer's pro-rata share at each disbursement and pushes payment directly (ACH or equivalent). Payment details and disbursement mechanics are specified in the signed agreement.

## The Already-Funded Project Advantage

The "safe" pitch in the portfolio is projects with existing institutional backing: affiliated university labs, grant-funded research programs, established researchers who need a specific resource to complete work that is already underway. These are not moonshots. A project with Brown University affiliation and existing grant funding that needs $519 for an Osmo Pocket 3 has a fundamentally different risk profile than a cold startup.

Leading with these de-risked projects builds the lab's track record. That track record makes the earlier-stage projects easier to pitch. This is the flywheel on the fundraising side.

The current grant funding and institutional investment climate is dire. Researchers cannot find money. This model gives them an alternative channel that is faster, more direct, and aligned with genuine believers in the work rather than institutional gatekeepers.

## Revenue Flow

### Step 1: Lab cut, immediately

Every dollar of revenue that comes in, the lab takes 15% off the top right away. The lab invested first. It gets paid first, every time.

### Step 2: Accumulate runway

The remaining 85% accumulates in the project account. No distributions to anyone until the account holds 3 to 6 months of operating expenses. The exact threshold is set at project launch and documented in the agreement.

### Step 3: Distributions begin

Once the runway threshold is hit, the first personnel disbursements go out. This moment starts year 1 for financial believers (12 rolling months from first disbursement, not from investment date). From this point, every new dollar of the 85% distributes immediately across the pools.

**If the project never hits its runway threshold:** Early believer contributions are a loss. Money has been spent building the project. The lab's responsibility is to select projects that make it. This must be stated clearly in all public-facing materials.

## Distribution Structure

### Layer 1: Lab Cut (15%, immediate)

Splits into two internal pools:

**Operations reserve:** Legal, IP filing, patenting, infrastructure, network costs

**Core pool:** Lab-wide roles (Executive Director, Director of Strategy and Partnerships, Lab Manager, Legal, Sales, HR). Each earns from the core pool proportionally by engagement points earned in the quarter. No fixed salary until the lab can sustain it. Points reset each quarter so compensation always reflects current contribution, not historical presence.

The Executive Director's share comes from the core pool. Their domain is the lab itself.

### Core Pool Points System

All core pool roles earn points through a unified engagement currency. Points are not dollars, they are units of contribution. At disbursement, each person's share of the pool equals their points divided by total points earned across all roles that quarter.

**Base points (per active month):**

| Role | Base pts/month |
|---|---|
| Executive Director | 40 |
| Director of Strategy and Partnerships | 20 |
| Lab Manager | 20 |
| Legal | 15 |
| HR | 15 |
| Sales | 10 |

**Activity bonuses (earned on top of base):**

| Role | Activity | Bonus pts |
|---|---|---|
| Director of Strategy | Partnership or affiliation closed | +5 |
| Sales | Per $1,000 raised (gift tier) | +2 |
| Sales | Per $1,000 raised ($1,000+ tier) | +3 |
| Legal | Agreement executed | +10 |
| Legal | Patent filed | +15 |
| HR | Collaborator onboarded | +5 |
| Lab Manager | Quarterly project review completed | +5 |
| Executive Director | Project milestone hit (per project) | +varies |

Sales earns more points per dollar on the higher tier because those agreements require more relationship work. But points are always converted to a fraction of the core pool, so a sales person's payout scales with the lab's actual revenue, not a fixed commission rate. If the lab earns less, everyone earns less. Incentives are fully aligned.

**Quarterly reset:** Points do not carry forward. Each quarter is a fresh accounting of actual contribution. This prevents early members from coasting on historical accumulation while newer high-contributors are undercompensated.

**Example (one quarter, core pool = $8,000):**

| Role | Points earned | Share | Payout |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Director | 140 | 33% | $2,640 |
| Director of Strategy | 75 | 18% | $1,440 |
| Sales | 95 | 22% | $1,760 |
| Lab Manager | 65 | 15% | $1,200 |
| Legal | 55 | 13% | $1,040 |
| **Total** | **430** | **100%** | **$8,080*** |

*Rounding adjusted.*

The Executive Director's share comes from the core pool, not a separate domain leader allocation, because their domain is the lab itself.

### Layer 2: Project Distribution (85%)

| Pool | Default % of 85% | Approximate % of total |
|---|---|---|
| Domain leader | ~47% | ~40% |
| Time collaborator pool | ~35% | ~30% |
| Financial believer pool | ~18% | ~15% |

All splits are agreed and signed before disbursements begin. Each project is a separate agreement.

## Contributor Tiers

Tiers are defined at project start, locked in at acceptance, and determine weight within the time collaborator pool.

| Tier | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Core collaborator | 1.0x | Daily involvement, load-bearing work |
| Contributing collaborator | 0.6x | Regular involvement, specific domain |
| Advisor | 0.25x | Periodic, specialized input |

**Vesting and performance review:** Collaborator weights vest on a quarterly schedule. The lab (via the Executive Director) conducts quarterly reviews for all active projects. Weight can be upgraded by agreement. Consistent non-performance results in a weight reduction or exit, documented and agreed in the original project agreement. This must be written into every project agreement at start.

## Domain Leader Exit Policy

**Exit before profit disbursements begin:**
Forfeits pool. Lab retains all IP. Remaining team or lab determines whether project continues.

**Exit after profit disbursements begin:**
Keeps what has already been paid. Must name a successor for the project to continue under the lab model. Lab retains IP regardless.

IP transfer to the lab is set at project launch and is non-negotiable.

**Project shutdown:**
If a project fails to reach its runway threshold and shuts down, it exits the lab, surrenders IP, and any remaining assets go to the lab. For investors this is always a gamble. The general fund is the safer bet (whole portfolio, lower individual upside) vs. a specific project (higher upside, higher risk). Structurally: individual stocks vs. the S&P 500.

---

## General Fund: The Lab as Portfolio

Backing the general fund is backing the lab itself as a project. Same mechanics as any project: 3-6 month runway threshold, year 1 window from first lab-level disbursements, loss if the lab fails before trigger.

Lower upside than a specific project. Exposure to the whole portfolio. The lab sets and can cap the general fund believer pool at its discretion.

---

## The Infinite Equity Property

Revenue participation rights are time-bounded. They are not permanent equity. So:

- Project raises early believer funding before launch
- Year 1 window opens once runway is stable
- Rights expire after year 1. Full ownership reverts to the team.
- Next round (if needed) starts fresh with no dilution

The company can close the window and move to a traditional funding round at any time.

## Sales and Outreach

The lab's sales and partnerships team are lab members compensated through the core pool points system, not commissioned salespeople. This distinction matters legally.

For the gift tier (under $1,000): team members describe the work and direct people to the project page. No securities activity.

For the $1,000+ tier: team members have conversations with interested parties and explain the model. Because the $1,000+ option is not publicly advertised and transactions are direct private agreements (not solicited through a portal), this is relationship outreach, not regulated securities solicitation. Legal review needed to confirm this holds in all relevant jurisdictions.

The points system means sales is rewarded for results (more points per dollar raised) but always as a fraction of the lab's own revenue. No fixed commission rate. No separate compensation tied specifically to securities transactions.

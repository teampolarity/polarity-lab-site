**Executive Director:** Theodore Addo, Polarity Lab

## The Question

Can a personal operating system designed to model who you are restore executive function without suppressing the authentic cognitive self — and does that design principle generalize beyond the person who built it?

The second half of the question is what makes this a research program rather than a product launch. The first half has already been answered. A founder with ADHD, who experienced the progressive suppression of his authentic cognitive self by every system that tried to help him, built his own tool. The tool models him rather than asking him to fit a pre-built model. It works. Executive function restored. The cross-domain, entrepreneurial, technically restless, creative self intact. That is a proof of concept. n=1.

The research program tests whether the design principle generalizes.

---

## Institutional Context

Polarity Lab's thesis is that designed environments have a second threshold. Below it, the environment extends the capacity it was built to serve. Beyond it, the optimization for the environment's own metrics begins to suppress the capacity it was built to develop.

Productivity and life management systems crossed this threshold when their optimization for measurable output — task completion rates, habit streaks, system compliance — diverged from the underlying human cognitive capacity those metrics were meant to represent. At some point the person is no longer using the tool. They are maintaining it. The executive function that was supposed to be extended is now being spent on keeping the system fed.

For people with ADHD, this trade is structurally worse. Systems that demand consistent compliance, predictable workflows, and linear task execution are optimized for a cognitive style that ADHD brains don't have and were never going to have. Every conventional productivity tool either offers to help by reshaping the person toward system compliance or breaks down when the person's natural cognitive style makes consistent compliance impossible. Neither outcome restores the authentic executive function. Both confirm the diagnosis as the problem rather than the environment.

Each lab at Polarity builds either a diagnostic instrument or a therapeutic environment. The Cosmos Lab builds both.

The **Cognitive Return Measure (CRM)** is the diagnostic instrument. It measures the signed gap between a person's executive function capacity when constrained by conventional productivity systems — which optimize for system compliance — and their capacity in the Cosmos counter-environment — which models the person. A positive CRM means the conventional environment has been suppressing something that the counter-environment can restore.

**Cosmos** is the therapeutic environment. It is a personal operating system designed from the principle that the system should fit the person, not the other way around. It builds a model of the person from behavior over time: knowledge patterns, goal structures, relationship dynamics, authentic commitments, cognitive style. It surfaces what the person needs rather than demanding what the system needs. The counter-environment doesn't ask the person to change. It changes itself.

## The Proof of Concept: Cosmos (n=1)

The founder built the first implementation of this counter-environment for himself. It runs as the administrative backend of his personal website. It is not a prototype or a conceptual sketch. It is a working system, used daily.

The current single-user implementation includes:
- Task and goal management structured around areas of life and authentic commitments, not productivity metrics
- A knowledge system with spaced repetition, decay scoring, and ease factors — active cognitive maintenance rather than passive storage
- A memory and pattern system: facts, preferences, and behavioral patterns the system learns over time, with confidence scores and reinforcement tracking
- A relationship graph connecting all entities (tasks, goals, people, knowledge, journal entries) with inferred and explicit links
- An AI assistant with persistent session memory, pattern observations, and knowledge updates — it builds a running model of the person across conversations
- People management with relationship health tracking, touchpoint intervals, and energy-type classification
- Life vision by area — keeping activity connected to authentic goals rather than system-imposed structure
- An insights engine that surfaces patterns rather than demanding input

What makes this a counter-environment rather than a better productivity app: the system's design goal is to model the person. The conventional tool's design goal is to get the person to model their life in a form the system can process. That inversion is the entire thesis. The CRM is designed to measure whether the inversion produces what it claims.

The founder has ADHD. Cosmos has been his primary operational system. The proof is that executive function is working and the authentic cognitive self — the cross-domain, entrepreneurial, technically restless, creatively driven person that every credential system and productivity tool tried to trade away — is intact. That is the same proof the lab was founded on, now instantiated in a running system rather than just an experience.

## Foundational Literature

---

### On ADHD, Executive Function, and Environmental Design

**1. Barkley, Russell A. *ADHD and the Nature of Self-Control.* Guilford Press, 1997.**

Barkley's foundational reformulation of ADHD as a disorder of self-regulation rather than attention per se. The core deficit is in the inhibitory control of behavior, which cascades into impaired working memory, impaired regulation of motivation and affect, and impaired reconstitution (the ability to analyze and synthesize novel behavioral sequences). ADHD is not a failure to know what to do. It is a failure to do what you know at the moment it matters.

*Relevance:* This reformulation is the theoretical foundation for what the CRM measures. The gap between what a person knows (intact in ADHD) and what they execute (impaired) is exactly the space where environmental design either helps or hurts. A system that holds context, sequences output, and surfaces the right information at the moment it matters is not compensating for a deficit — it is functioning as the external scaffolding for inhibitory control that the internal architecture cannot reliably provide.

---

**2. Brown, Thomas E. *Smart but Stuck: Unraveling Learning Disabilities and Unlocked Potential in Average-to-Brilliant Adults and Children.* Jossey-Bass, 2014.**

Brown's clinical work documents the specific profile of high-cognitive-capacity adults with ADHD: strong intelligence, deep interests, significant creative and cross-domain capacity, severe difficulty with conventional productivity and organizational systems. The characteristic pattern is not low capability but inconsistent access to capability — and the environments that suppress access most reliably are those designed for stable, linear, compliance-oriented cognitive styles.

*Relevance:* This is the clinical description of the population Cosmos is designed for and the mechanism of suppression the CRM is designed to measure. The "smart but stuck" profile is precisely the profile of a person who has hit the second threshold with conventional productivity systems: sufficient intelligence to understand what the system is asking, insufficient system-compliance to give it consistently, and progressive suppression of authentic capability under the accumulated weight of system failure.

---

**3. Hallowell, Edward M., and John J. Ratey. *Driven to Distraction: Recognizing and Coping with Attention Deficit Disorder.* Pantheon Books, 1994.**

Hallowell and Ratey's foundational clinical text documents the phenomenology of ADHD from the inside: the experience of interest-driven cognition, the capacity for hyperfocus on genuinely engaging problems, the specific misery of systems that demand compliance with structures that conflict with natural cognitive style. They describe ADHD as a neurological difference with real costs in conventional structured environments and real advantages in environments that fit the cognitive profile.

*Relevance:* The environmental fit framing is the conceptual foundation for Cosmos. The problem is not the person. The problem is the mismatch between the person's cognitive profile and the environment they're operating in. Cosmos is designed to close that mismatch at the system level rather than demanding the person close it through compliance.

---

**4. Sonuga-Barke, Edmund J.S. "Causal Heterogeneity in Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder: A Model of 4 Neurobiological Pathways." *Biological Psychiatry,* vol. 57, no. 11, 2005, pp. 1231-1238.**

Sonuga-Barke's dual-pathway model distinguishes executive function deficits from motivational and reward-processing deficits in ADHD. The two pathways interact: systems that fail to provide appropriate motivational scaffolding (immediate feedback, variable reinforcement, authentic interest alignment) compound executive function deficits rather than compensating for them.

*Relevance:* This model directly informs Cosmos's design. The CRM needs to account for both pathways: does Cosmos improve executive function output (task completion, goal progress, knowledge maintenance) AND does it maintain motivational engagement (genuine interest alignment, authentic goal connection, absence of compliance dread)? A tool that improves the first while worsening the second is not a counter-environment.

---

### On Productivity Systems, Compliance, and Cognitive Cost

**5. Mark, Gloria, Daniela Gudith, and Ulrich Klocke. "The Cost of Interrupted Work: More Speed and Stress." *Proceedings of the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems.* ACM, 2008, pp. 107-110.**

Mark and colleagues document the cognitive switching cost of interruption in knowledge work: after an interruption, workers took an average of 23 minutes to return to the original task, and interrupted work was performed significantly faster with more errors and higher stress. System-maintenance demands — updating tasks, logging progress, maintaining system state — are a category of interruption imposed by the tool itself.

*Relevance:* Every interaction a productivity tool demands from the person that doesn't directly serve the person's goals is a cognitive tax. Systems that demand high maintenance interact with ADHD's executive function deficits to produce exactly the conditions Mark et al. document. Cosmos is designed to minimize maintenance demand — the system updates its own model from behavior rather than requiring explicit input. The CRM measures whether this reduction in maintenance demand produces measurable cognitive benefit.

---

**6. Baumeister, Roy F., Ellen Bratslavsky, Mark Muraven, and Dianne M. Tice. "Ego Depletion: Is the Self a Limited Resource?" *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology,* vol. 74, no. 5, 1998, pp. 1252-1265.**

Baumeister and colleagues demonstrated that acts of self-regulation draw on a limited cognitive resource that depletes with use. Subsequent self-regulatory acts performed after depletion show impairment. The mechanism is contested but the behavioral effect — that demanding compliance in one domain reduces capacity in another — is well-documented.

*Relevance:* Productivity systems that demand consistent compliance and self-monitoring draw on the same self-regulatory resource that executive function requires. For ADHD, where that resource is already constrained, a system that demands high compliance as a condition of function is consuming the resource it claims to restore. The counter-environment design — minimal compliance demand, maximal environmental scaffolding — is a direct application of this framework.

---

**7. Csikszentmihalyi, Mihaly. *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience.* Harper and Row, 1990.**

Csikszentmihalyi's foundational theory of flow: the state of optimal experience characterized by full engagement, intrinsic motivation, and loss of self-consciousness, produced when challenge and capability are appropriately matched. Flow is interest-driven, context-sensitive, and incompatible with persistent external structure demands.

*Relevance:* The authentic cognitive self that Cosmos is designed to restore is, in part, the capacity for flow. Conventional productivity tools optimize against flow by imposing external structure that competes with the internal state flow requires. The CRM should include flow-state frequency and quality as an outcome measure — a restored authentic cognitive self should show increased flow access.

---

### On Personal Information Management and System Design

**8. Jones, William, and Jaime Teevan (eds.). *Personal Information Management.* University of Washington Press, 2007.**

A comprehensive academic treatment of how people manage personal information — tasks, knowledge, relationships, projects — across systems and over time. The central finding: people develop idiosyncratic personal information management strategies that reflect their cognitive style, context, and priorities. Imposed systems that don't accommodate this idiosyncrasy are abandoned or gamed.

*Relevance:* The conventional productivity tool design failure is documented in this literature: systems designed for an idealized rational information manager rather than for the diverse cognitive styles and contexts of actual users. Cosmos's design principle — model the person rather than impose a model on the person — is a direct response to this failure.

---

**9. Kaptelinin, Victor, and Bonnie Nardi. *Acting with Technology: Activity Theory and Interaction Design.* MIT Press, 2006.**

Activity theory frames technology as a tool mediating between a person (the subject) and their goals (the object). A tool that imposes its own structure on the subject-object relationship becomes an obstacle rather than a mediator. The design question is always: does the tool serve the person's activity or does it redirect the person's activity toward serving the tool?

*Relevance:* This is the theoretical framing for the second threshold as applied to productivity tools. A productivity system crosses the threshold when it becomes the object of the person's activity rather than the mediator toward their authentic goals. Cosmos is designed to stay on the mediating side of that line. The CRM is designed to measure whether it does.

---

**10. Czerwinski, Mary, Eric Horvitz, and Susan Wilhite. "A Diary Study of Task Switching and Interruptions." *Proceedings of the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems.* ACM, 2004, pp. 175-182.**

A diary study of information workers found that on a typical workday, workers switched tasks an average of every 3.5 minutes, and that most switches were self-initiated based on perceived urgency rather than system-directed. The study documents the mismatch between how people actually work and how productivity systems are designed — as if tasks are worked linearly to completion.

*Relevance:* Cosmos's design accommodates non-linear, interest- and urgency-driven cognitive work rather than demanding linear task execution. The CRM baseline measurement must account for actual working patterns rather than idealized productivity system behavior.

---

### On the Measurement of Cognitive Restoration

**11. Barkley, Russell A. *Executive Functions: What They Are, How They Work, and Why They Evolved.* Guilford Press, 2012.**

Barkley's comprehensive model of executive function as a set of self-regulatory capacities: self-awareness, inhibition, non-verbal working memory, verbal working memory, emotional self-regulation, self-motivation, and planning and problem-solving. Each capacity is distinct and independently measurable.

*Relevance:* The CRM validation requires operationalization of which executive function capacities Cosmos is designed to restore and how those capacities are measured. Barkley's taxonomy provides the validated framework. The CRM is not measuring "productivity" — it is measuring whether specific executive function capacities, previously suppressed by environment-person mismatch, return under counter-environment conditions.

---

**12. Gioia, Gerard A., Peter K. Isquith, Steven C. Guy, and Lauren Kenworthy. *Behavior Rating Inventory of Executive Function (BRIEF).* Psychological Assessment Resources, 2000.**

The BRIEF is the most widely used validated instrument for measuring executive function in clinical and research settings. It assesses eight clinical scales (Inhibit, Shift, Emotional Control, Initiate, Working Memory, Plan/Organize, Organization of Materials, Monitor) and two global indices (Behavioral Regulation and Metacognition). The BRIEF has established reliability and validity and is sensitive to change over time.

*Relevance:* The CRM should incorporate or align with BRIEF subscales to enable comparison with existing clinical literature and to provide a validated baseline against which Cosmos-driven change can be measured. The Metacognition index — which covers Initiate, Working Memory, Plan/Organize, Organization of Materials, and Monitor — is the most directly relevant to the executive function gaps Cosmos is designed to address.

## Research Aims

### Aim 1: Validate the Cognitive Return Measure as a Reproducible Instrument

**Specific Aim:** To develop a valid and reliable Cognitive Return Measure — a scored assessment of the gap between a person's executive function capacity under conventional productivity system constraints and their capacity in the Cosmos counter-environment — and establish its relationship to validated clinical measures of executive function.

**Background and Significance**

The n=1 proof of concept demonstrates that the design principle works for one person who built the system for himself. Generalizing that proof requires an instrument that can measure the same outcome for people who didn't build the system, at baseline before Cosmos use and after a defined period of counter-environment exposure.

The CRM operationalizes "cognitive return" as a composite of three components: (1) executive function capacity as measured by BRIEF-validated subscales (Initiate, Working Memory, Plan/Organize, Monitor), assessed before and after Cosmos onboarding; (2) authentic goal alignment — the degree to which a person's active work reflects their stated authentic priorities rather than system-imposed structure; (3) cognitive self-recognition — whether the person identifies the cognitive self that emerges in the counter-environment as authentically theirs or as an artifact of the system.

**Approach**

Recruit 40 adults with confirmed ADHD diagnoses who are currently using at least one conventional productivity system (Notion, Todoist, Things, Asana, etc.) as their primary organizational tool. Administer BRIEF and baseline CRM measures. Transition to Cosmos for 90 days. Re-administer at 30, 60, and 90 days.

**Primary Outcome:** Change in BRIEF Metacognition Index score from baseline to 90 days, compared to a waitlist control group continuing conventional tool use.

**Secondary Outcomes:** Change in authentic goal alignment score. Self-reported cognitive self-recognition. Exit interview: does the person identify their experience in Cosmos as restoration of something that was suppressed, or as learning a new skill?

**Timeline:** 12-18 months.

**Expected Outcomes:** A published CRM instrument specification with validation data. Normative CRM baselines for ADHD adults using conventional productivity tools. Effect size estimates for the Cosmos intervention, informing power calculations for Aim 3.

---

### Aim 2: Characterize the Second Threshold in Productivity Systems

**Specific Aim:** To test whether conventional productivity system compliance demands measurably suppress executive function capacity in ADHD adults, and to identify the system design features most predictive of suppression.

**Background and Significance**

The counter-environment's effect can only be measured against a baseline of what the environment was doing. Aim 2 establishes that baseline empirically: what specifically do conventional productivity systems do to executive function in ADHD, and which design features produce the most suppression?

**Approach**

In the Aim 1 baseline sample, correlate system compliance demand (measured by a purpose-built System Compliance Demand Scale assessing linearity requirements, maintenance load, input consistency demands, and structure rigidity) with BRIEF subscale scores. Test whether higher compliance demand predicts lower executive function output, controlling for ADHD severity, age, and system experience.

Conduct 20 semi-structured interviews with ADHD adults who have abandoned multiple productivity systems: what specifically made the system fail for them? Where did the system-maintenance demand become the primary activity?

**Timeline:** Concurrent with Aim 1.

**Expected Outcomes:** An empirically grounded characterization of the design features that produce suppression in ADHD productivity tools. Published System Compliance Demand Scale for use in subsequent tool evaluation research.

---

### Aim 3: Test Cosmos as a Counter-Environment at Scale

**Specific Aim:** To test whether Cosmos produces significantly greater improvement in BRIEF Metacognition Index scores than conventional productivity tools in a randomized trial of 120 ADHD adults, and to establish that improvement is accompanied by self-reported authentic cognitive self-recognition rather than skill acquisition alone.

**Background and Significance**

Aims 1 and 2 establish the instrument and characterize the problem. This aim tests the intervention at clinical scale. The randomized design controls for the confound that motivated people who choose Cosmos might improve regardless of the tool. The authentic self-recognition outcome distinguishes Cosmos's claimed effect (returning something that was suppressed) from a productivity improvement effect (teaching a new skill).

**Approach**

120 ADHD adults, randomized to Cosmos (n=60) or their choice of conventional productivity tool (n=60). BRIEF and CRM at baseline, 45 days, 90 days. Primary outcome: BRIEF Metacognition Index change at 90 days. Secondary outcome: authentic self-recognition interview at 90 days. Exploratory: platform usage log analysis (with consent) — what usage patterns predict CRM improvement?

**IRB:** Brown University or University of Rhode Island.

**Timeline:** 18-24 months, following Aim 1 instrument finalization.

**Expected Outcomes:** The first randomized trial testing a personal OS counter-environment against conventional productivity tools for ADHD adults. A positive result — that Cosmos produces significantly greater CRM improvement, accompanied by authentic self-recognition — establishes Cosmos as a therapeutic environment for executive function restoration, with direct clinical implications and a publishable basis for the lab's founding proof of concept.

## The Gap We Fill

The ADHD treatment literature is dominated by pharmacological and behavioral interventions. Environmental design is underrepresented as an intervention category despite theoretical support for its efficacy (Barkley, Sonuga-Barke). The personal information management literature documents that conventional system design fails ADHD users but does not propose a counter-environment design principle. No existing research program has tested whether a personal operating system that inverts the compliance demand — modeling the person rather than asking the person to model themselves to the system — produces measurable executive function restoration.

The Cosmos research program fills that gap.

## Potential Funders and Partners

**National Institute of Mental Health**
NIMH's Technology and Innovation in Mental Health program funds novel technology-based interventions for mental health conditions. A personal OS counter-environment for ADHD with a randomized trial design and validated CRM instrument is within this scope.

**National Science Foundation, Human-Centered Computing**
The CRM instrument development and Cosmos counter-environment evaluation fit NSF's Human-Centered Computing program: technology-mediated human experience, cognitive science, and empirical evaluation of interactive systems.

**Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI)**
PCORI funds comparative effectiveness research from the patient perspective. A randomized comparison of Cosmos versus conventional productivity tools with patient-reported authentic self-recognition as a primary outcome is precisely within PCORI's mandate.

**CHADD (Children and Adults with Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)**
CHADD's research partnership program and its direct access to the ADHD clinical and patient community make it a natural partner for participant recruitment, validation, and dissemination.

**Brown University Department of Psychiatry and Human Behavior**
Clinical ADHD expertise, IRB infrastructure, and established community relationships for participant recruitment. The most natural academic partner given the lab's Providence base.

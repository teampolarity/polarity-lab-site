## What You're Here For

WAXFEED exists because authentic musical taste is one of the clearest places to see what algorithmic environments suppress and what recovery looks like when you design against the suppression. You run this project because you have lived that. Not as an observer. As the subject.

Your blog documents the mechanism precisely from the inside. The social environment optimizing for frictionless interaction. The corrections exacted until the corrections become the identity. The secondary life that forms to protect what the primary layer can no longer show. The raw expression breaking through because the authentic self didn't disappear — it went underground.

WAXFEED is the musical proof of exactly that. The question is not "what does the algorithm think you like." The question is "what do you actually like" and "what has three years of recommendation suppressed." The instrument measures the gap. The platform is built to close it.

That's what you're building and why you're the one building it.

---

## What WAXFEED Is

A music discovery platform that builds a cognitive profile from listening behavior, measuring the gap between what the recommendation environment predicts you want and what your genuine taste response actually is. The platform is the counter-environment. The Cognitive Gap Measure is the instrument. The data they generate together is the research.

This is not a streaming service with better recommendations. It is a proof — that authentic musical identity survives algorithmic suppression and can be surfaced back to the person who lost it. The before-and-after is the deliverable. To the user, and to the research record.

## The Cognitive Gap Measure

The instrument has to be defined precisely before the platform can generate meaningful data. At minimum, the Cognitive Gap Measure needs to specify:

- What constitutes an "authentic taste response" as distinct from a conditioned one
- How the platform distinguishes a genuine preference signal from habituated engagement
- What the "gap" is being measured against — predicted preference, historical listening, or something else
- How much behavioral data is needed before the gap measurement becomes meaningful
- What "closing the gap" looks like in the data — what that would prove and what it would not

This is the research design question at the center of the project. The technical prototype needs to be built around the answer to it, not the other way around.

## What to Work On Right Now

**Define the Cognitive Gap Measure precisely.** Before more platform gets built, the instrument needs a clear specification. What it's measuring, how it measures it, and what a valid result looks like. This is the research design work that makes everything else downstream meaningful.

**Technical prototype to instrument-ready state.** The prototype exists. The next milestone is getting it to the point where it can generate behavioral data that the Cognitive Gap Measure can actually run on. That's the threshold that makes WAXFEED's research claim credible.

Define how data is collected, anonymized, and structured for licensing.

**Research affiliation.** Map who the right potential partners are -- music cognition researchers, information systems faculty, algorithmic auditing groups at MIT or Brown. Brief the Director of Strategy. Strategy runs the first outreach. You provide the scientific depth once there's a real conversation.

**Gift tier production.** The custom vinyl record production path via Untitled.app needs to be operationally ready before early believers at the gift tier are accepted. Confirm the production timeline and what the artifact actually is.

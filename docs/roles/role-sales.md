## What You're Here For

The lab's early believers don't find the work through ads. They find it through people who understand the work well enough to describe it honestly. That's this role. You're building the community of people who believe before it's obvious — not through pitch tactics, but through genuine description of what the lab is doing and why it matters.

That community is the lab's early infrastructure. Gift tier believers before the products launch. People who back WAXFEED before the platform exists, GPS before Providence is live, IΔ before the paper is published. Every person who backs the work early is a signal that the vision is landing with real people. That signal matters to the team, to institutional partners, and eventually to the aligned investors the lab is choosing between.

The commercial instinct is appropriate here. The lab needs believers and your job is to find them. But the lane is specific and there are real legal boundaries on either side of it. Read this document carefully before any conversation with a prospective backer.

---

## What You Do

You describe the work and direct people to the right place. For most conversations, this means explaining what the lab is, what the active projects are doing, and directing interested people to the project pages where they can contribute at the gift tier. No financial return is promised or implied at this tier. No securities conversation happens. You're telling people about work worth believing in and making it easy to act on that belief.

You build the pipeline of prospective gift tier believers. Who is the right audience for each project? IΔ attracts people in clinical AI, medicine, health tech, and AI safety. WAXFEED attracts music people, the cognitively curious, anyone who's ever noticed that their Spotify recommendations stopped surprising them. GPS attracts Providence-based people, urban discovery enthusiasts, people interested in cities and culture. AVDP attracts documentary audiences, media-critical people, format experimenters. You understand each project's audience well enough to find the right people for it.

You surface $1,000+ prospects for the Director of Strategy. The $1,000+ tier is not yours to lead -- those conversations are relationship-based, not publicly advertised, and run by the Director of Strategy within terms set by the founder. If a prospective backer at that tier surfaces through your outreach, your job is to bring them to the Director of Strategy's attention. You may be in the room in a support capacity. You don't conduct those conversations independently.

You track what's working. Which outreach is converting. Which audiences are responding. Which project descriptions are landing. This information is useful to the whole team. Keep it.

## The Legal Lane

This section is not optional reading. The two-track contribution structure has real legal boundaries and your role sits on a specific side of them.

**Gift tier (under $1,000):** Describing the work, directing people to project pages, building community. No financial return is promised. No expectation of one. This is structurally the same as how Kickstarter campaigns operate. No securities activity occurs at this tier.

**$5,000+ tier:** This tier involves revenue participation agreements that are structured as private securities offerings under Reg D Rule 506(b). That means no general solicitation. The $5,000+ option is not publicly advertised on the site. The financial terms are discussed only in private conversations initiated by the prospective backer, led by the Director of Strategy.

Your compensation comes through the core pool points system, not commission. That structure is part of what removes the broker-dealer obligation for your role. Do not accept payment, promises of payment, or finder's fees tied specifically to $1,000+ agreements from any party. That crosses a legal line. The Director of Strategy's compensation works differently -- but that's their situation to manage, not yours.

The legal brief has the full structure. Counsel is actively reviewing Question 10 specifically -- whether the points-based compensation structure fully removes the broker-dealer registration obligation for individuals who participate in $1,000+ conversations in a support capacity. Until that review is complete, do not lead those conversations. Bring interested parties to the Director of Strategy.

If you're ever unsure which tier a conversation is heading toward, stop and ask the founder before continuing.

## What Stays With the Founder and Director of Strategy

$1,000+ conversations are led by the Director of Strategy, within terms set by the founder. Always.

Which projects are active and what they're doing is the lab's to communicate. You describe the work as it is. You don't make claims about future milestones, expected returns, or product timelines beyond what the lab has actually committed to publicly.

Institutional partnerships and grant relationships belong to the Director of Strategy and Partnerships. Your outreach is to individuals, not organizations.

## How You Get Paid

You earn points through the core pool each quarter. Your base is 10 points per active month. You earn activity bonuses on results: 2 points per $1,000 raised at the gift tier, 3 points per $1,000 raised at the $5,000+ tier.

The higher bonus at the $1,000+ tier reflects that those conversations require more relationship work. But the points still convert to a fraction of the core pool — if the lab earns less, everyone earns less. There is no fixed commission rate. No separate compensation tied specifically to any individual agreement.

Your share of the core pool each quarter equals your points divided by total points earned across all core pool roles. Points reset each quarter.

This doesn't start until the lab hits its runway threshold. The full mechanics are in polarity-fund-model.md.

## Ownership and Governance

Polarity Lab LLC is Theodore's. You are a collaborator, not an owner. You have no governance rights over the lab's direction, research agenda, or any project.

This role has a specific legal footprint. The single-member structure, the points-based compensation model, and the no-general-solicitation design of the $1,000+ tier are all load-bearing. Don't improvise around them.

If anything here doesn't match your understanding of your role, that conversation happens with the founder before any outreach begins.

**Map the audience for each project.** IΔ, WAXFEED, GPS, AVDP — each one has a different community of potential early believers. Where do those people exist? What are they reading, who are they following, what communities are they already in? Build that map before any outreach starts.

**Coordinate with the Director of Strategy on outreach positioning.** Strategy owns institutional relationships, $1,000+ conversations, and the broader partnership pipeline. You own individual gift-tier outreach. There's natural overlap -- you may surface someone who belongs in the $1,000+ track. When that happens, hand them off clearly and don't continue the conversation independently. Make sure you're not duplicating effort or sending different messages to the same people.

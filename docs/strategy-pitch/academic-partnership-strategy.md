## Why This Document Exists

Three grant outlines (Knight/GPS, NIMH/IΔ+Cosmos, Mozilla/WAXFEED) share a common bottleneck: each requires an academic institutional partner before the application can be submitted or, in the NIMH case, before the grant is fundable at all. This document maps which partnerships are needed, which are blocking vs. accelerating, who to approach, in what order, and what the ask looks like for each.

Academic partnerships are slow. Typical timeline from first conversation to signed agreement is 3-6 months. Starting these conversations late means grants slip by one or two cycles. Starting them now means the first applications are ready when the next cycle opens.

## Priority Stack

**Priority 1 — NIMH academic partner (blocking)**
This is the only partnership where the absence of a partner prevents submission entirely. NIMH will not fund a small independent research organization as the prime awardee on an R01 or R21. The Executive Director can be named as a co-investigator, but an academic PI with NIH track record must be the lead. Identifying this person is the pre-application task with the most leverage.

**Priority 2 — IRB infrastructure (blocking for controlled studies)**
Aims 3 across all three grants require IRB approval for human subjects research. Polarity Lab cannot independently apply for IRB approval without an institutional home. An academic partner who provides both the PI credentials (NIMH) and the IRB infrastructure (all three grants) is the single most valuable partnership the lab can secure.

**Priority 3 — Credibility partners (accelerating)**
For Knight and Mozilla, an academic co-PI strengthens the proposal and demonstrates methodological rigor but is not required for submission. These partnerships can be initiated in parallel with Priority 1 and 2 outreach, but should not delay application if not secured.

**Target contacts:**
- Brown Psychiatry and Human Behavior department: look for faculty working on ADHD digital interventions, AI in clinical settings, or executive function measurement
- Brown School of Public Health: look for faculty working on community health equity, health information access, or digital health disparities
- Carney Institute for Brain Science: relevant for longer-term neural synchronization aims, not blocking for current grants

- NIMH application: co-PI agreement; Brown as prime institution with Polarity Lab as subawardee; IRB sponsorship

## What Each Partner Conversation Needs

### The research question first

Every first conversation should start with the scientific problem, not the grant opportunity. Lead with what the lab is measuring and why it matters. The IΔ is the strongest hook for clinical academic partners: sycophantic AI suppresses correct psychiatric diagnosis in the models community mental health centers are deploying. The GPS is the strongest hook for public health partners: the MUSEOFRI Pain Points Report and GOODhumanz Closing the Gap whitepaper document that BIPOC-led nonprofit resources are systematically invisible — the Proximity Index is the instrument designed to score and reproduce that finding formally.

### The honest ask

Be explicit about what the lab needs from the partner:
- NIMH: Lead PI credit; institutional affiliation; IRB sponsorship. In exchange: the scientific idea, the instrument design, the pilot data, and the grant writing.
- Knight/Mozilla: Subcontract for statistical analysis and methodology review; IRB sponsorship for human subjects. In exchange: the scientific question, the instrument, the data collection, and a co-authored publication.

Do not over-promise lab resources or under-state what the partner is being asked to provide.

## What Polarity Lab Brings to Every Partnership

This is the negotiation context. Academic partners are trading their institutional infrastructure for:

1. **Genuinely novel instruments.** The CGM, CRM, IΔ, Proximity Index, SAM, and FEM are not incremental extensions of existing measures. They measure constructs no validated instrument currently captures. An academic co-PI on any of these gets their name on the validation paper for the instrument.

2. **Pilot data.** The IΔ has n=5 pilot data. GPS has a live platform at polaritygps.com and an established community partnership with MUSEOFRI and GOODhumanz -- the formal Proximity Index pilot measurement is the next step, not a completed result. These are preliminary assets that most academic researchers would need 12-18 months to generate themselves.

3. **Live deployed platforms.** Polarity GPS (polaritygps.com) and WAXFEED exist. An academic partner does not need to wait for the counter-environment to be built. They are co-PI on a study of something that is running.

4. **Community partnerships.** MUSEOFRI and GOODhumanz are established partner organizations in Providence. An academic partner inherits those relationships and the participant recruitment infrastructure.

5. **Grant writing.** The lab writes the grants. The academic partner reviews and signs as PI. The time burden on the academic partner is concentrated at the review, revision, and IRB stages, not the development stage.

## Risks

**Slow academic timelines.** University partnership and IRB processes can take 6-12 months even after a faculty partner is identified. Build this into grant cycle planning. If a partnership is not established by month 4, the NIMH February 2027 target may slip to October 2027.

**IP and publication disputes.** Academic partners typically expect publication rights and may expect co-ownership of instrument methodologies developed during the partnership. The collaborator agreement (and its IP transfer clause) applies to academic partners who contribute to instrument development. For advisory or PI-credential partners who are not contributing to instrument design, a simpler consulting or co-PI agreement with Polarity Lab owning the core methodology is appropriate. Legal review required before any co-PI agreement is signed.

**Institutional overhead.** Academic institutions typically add 40-60% indirect cost rates to grants they administer as prime. A $300,000 direct cost budget at Brown may require $420,000-$480,000 in total budget. This needs to be factored into grant budget planning. Mozilla and Knight have lower tolerance for indirect costs than NIH.

**Faculty availability and interest alignment.** Not every technically qualified faculty member will have the interest and bandwidth for a partnership of this kind. Expect to approach 4-6 faculty contacts to identify 1-2 genuine partnership conversations.

# Polarity Lab — Funding Strategy
*Living document. Read alongside monetization.md and strategy-intelligence.md.*

---

## THE CORE QUESTION

Should the lab seek funding? It depends entirely on what kind and from whom. The independence of the methodology is not a nice-to-have. It is the asset. The instruments are only worth something because no funder can tell the lab what to find. The moment that independence is in question, the methodology loses its value. Not gradually. Immediately.

Every funding decision gets evaluated against one test: does this compromise the independence growth loop? If yes, it destroys more value than it creates regardless of the capital amount.

---

## THE GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

Polarity Lab LLC has a single member: the founder. This is not a default. It is a deliberate structural decision made for the same reason every other structural decision in the lab was made. The algorithm lives in one person. The methodology's integrity depends on the vision not being diluted or redirected by people who don't yet fully understand it. A multi-member LLC with governance rights distributed across cofounders or early collaborators creates exactly the kind of dependency the lab was designed to prevent.

So: collaborators participate in the economics through project-level revenue participation agreements and the core pool points system. They do not hold governance rights over the lab's research agenda, methodology, or direction. Titles reflect function and contribution. Ownership and direction remain with the founder until the lab's vision has been fully internalized by someone capable of carrying it. And that transfer, if it ever happens, happens deliberately and on the lab's terms.

For investors: entering into a revenue participation agreement at the project level does not confer governance rights over the lab. The LLC structure makes this legally explicit, not just philosophically stated. When aligned investors like 1517 come in, they are backing the founder and the methodology. They are not acquiring a seat at the table where the research agenda is set.

This structure IS the independence constraint made legal.

---

## THE INDEPENDENCE GROWTH LOOP

This loop must stay intact through every funding decision:

Proof contracts generate revenue → revenue funds research without dilutive capital → research validates instruments → validated instruments attract better proof contracts → better contracts generate more revenue → independent revenue means the lab chooses its investors rather than needing them → selectivity protects the methodology's trustworthiness → trustworthiness is the asset everything depends on.

The loop compounds over time. Early-stage independence is the hardest to maintain and the most important to protect. A funder who compromises independence at the seed stage contaminates the methodology before it is established. There is no recovering from that.

## THREE CATEGORIES OF FUNDING

### Category 1: Non-dilutive funding — yes, aggressively

Grants don't take equity and don't require product success. They legitimize the research, don't compromise independence, and don't create the product-scale pressure that would distort the methodology. This is the SFI model. Intellectual authority generates grant revenue which funds more research which generates more intellectual authority.

**Priority targets:**

*Research grants:*
- NIH (National Institutes of Health): Clinical AI, deskilling, cognitive health. IΔ maps directly to existing funding priorities in AI safety in clinical settings.
- NSF (National Science Foundation): Human-centered computing, AI and society, information and intelligent systems.
- Wellcome Trust: Mental health, cognitive restoration, AI in medicine. Strong international reach relevant to DSA-facing research.
- Knight Foundation: Media, local communities, discovery infrastructure. Direct alignment with GPS and AVDP.
- MacArthur Foundation: Technology and society, independent research institutions.
- Mellon Foundation: Arts, culture, humanities. AVDP and GPS have strong fits here.
- Mozilla Foundation: Responsible tech, independent research. Direct alignment with the lab's structural independence.

*Institutional funding:*
- SFI (Santa Fe Institute) affiliate or visiting researcher relationships: the institutional parallel the lab is building toward. Being in conversation with SFI while building toward that model is strategically useful.
- DARPA: Long-shot but worth understanding. DARPA has funded cross-domain instrument-building before.
- European Research Council: Relevant given the DSA regulatory context. IΔ and Proximity Index have direct European regulatory relevance.

**Why grants come first before equity:** Non-dilutive funding establishes the research credibility that makes the methodology trustworthy to institutional buyers. NIH funding a paper is a signal to FDA reviewers. Wellcome Trust funding clinical AI research is a signal to hospital systems. The grant isn't just capital. It's institutional endorsement that makes the proof contracts easier to close.

### Category 2: Aligned early-stage investors — yes, selectively

There are investors whose model is compatible with the lab's independence constraint. They exist, but the profile is narrow. Every prospective investor gets evaluated against all four criteria below before any conversation progresses to terms.

**The investor profile:**
1. Backs founders over products. The investment thesis is the person and the methodology, not a specific product's growth curve.
2. Does not need standard VC returns on standard VC timelines. The standard licensing and institutional revenue channels are the path to returns, not consumer scale exits.
3. Has no portfolio conflicts. No investments in the environments the lab would be measuring or auditing.
4. Explicitly accepts the independence constraint. The lab's research findings cannot be influenced by the investor's interests. This is non-negotiable in any term sheet.

**1517 Fund as the model:**

1517's thesis: universities suppress what they claim to develop, and the people those institutions can't contain are worth backing. The founder fits the profile. An MD-creative who couldn't be contained, ADHD, built his own tools, crossed into tech and entrepreneurship because the system wouldn't hold him.

More importantly: the lab's thesis is the generalization of 1517's thesis. Universities are one designed environment that suppresses what they claim to develop. 1517 identified one instance of the phenomenon the lab is measuring across all instances. That's not a copy relationship. That's a foundational relationship. The pitch to 1517 is not "we're similar to you." It's: "You proved one case of the phenomenon we're building instruments for across every designed environment. I'm the proof of concept your thesis predicts. The lab is what comes after escape. It's proof you can design better environments."

**The 1517 pitch in full:**
"You proved that one institutional environment suppresses what it claims to develop and that the people it can't contain are the ones worth backing. I'm one of those people. I'm an MD-creative who hit the second threshold in medicine and in the creative world, built the tool that recovered my own capacity without altering who I am, and recognized the mechanism in every designed environment I'd ever entered. 1517 proved you can escape. I'm building the proof that you can design better environments. Cosmos is the first one. The other four are next."

**Term sheet non-negotiables:**
- No board control or veto rights over research agenda or instrument findings
- No rights to review or delay publication of research outputs
- No conflict-of-interest provisions that would restrict which environments the lab can study
- The independence constraint is explicit in the agreement, not just understood

### Category 3: Conventional VC — no

Standard venture capital is structurally incompatible with what the lab is. The reasons are specific, not philosophical.

**Why conventional VC breaks the lab:**

1. **Growth pressure distorts the methodology.** VC requires scale and growth. Growth pressure creates incentives to prioritize consumer acquisition over research integrity, to delay publishing findings that might slow commercial partnerships, to avoid auditing environments where partners have interests. Each of these decisions erodes the trustworthiness of the instruments. The methodology loses its value precisely when the commercial pressure is highest.

2. **Portfolio conflicts are unavoidable at scale.** Any VC fund with broad tech portfolio exposure almost certainly has investments in the types of environments the lab would be measuring. Social platforms, clinical AI vendors, recommendation systems. The moment a funder's portfolio company is a potential audit subject, the independence question is live. There is no structural resolution to this conflict at the VC fund level.

3. **Exit requirements are incompatible with the institutional form.** VC exits require either acquisition or IPO. Acquisition by a tech company or platform immediately contaminates the methodology. IPO creates public market pressures that produce the same distortions as growth-stage VC, at greater scale. The lab's value is in its independence. The exit events that VC requires destroy what they're buying.

4. **The algorithm cannot be acquired.** The founder is the algorithm. The methodology is inseparable from the lived experience that built it. A VC taking equity in the lab is not buying ownership of a codified system. They are buying a claim on a person's judgment and independence. That is a relationship with no clean resolution at exit.

**The only exception:** Project-level equity for specific products (WAXFEED, GPS as standalone entities) could accept conventional VC if the project is structurally separated from the lab's methodology business and the investment agreement makes that separation explicit and enforceable. The lab retains its 15% through the fund structure. The project can raise and scale under conventional VC constraints without contaminating the methodology.

## THE FIRST MOVE

The first move is not fundraising. It is the first proof contract.

One IΔ assessment for one clinical AI vendor generates revenue from the methodology itself, validates the instrument, creates the citation trail, and demonstrates the proof contract model. Without giving anyone equity. Without creating any pressure that conflicts with the research. Without needing consumer scale.

Walking into 1517 or any aligned investor having already performed the trick once for a paying institutional buyer is a categorically different conversation from walking in with a pitch deck. The first proof contract transforms the ask from "believe we can" to "we already did." That changes the terms, the valuation, and the selectivity the lab can exercise.

Generate revenue from the methodology first. Then raise from a position of proof, not promise.

## HOW THE INTERNAL STRUCTURE PROTECTS INDEPENDENCE

The full mechanics of how the lab is invested in, including disbursement layers, contributor tiers, the financial believer model, the points system, and legal tracks, live in `docs/polarity-fund-model.md`. Read that document for the operational detail. What belongs here is why the structure was designed the way it was, and how it maps to the independence constraint.

**The 15% lab cut is non-negotiable for a specific reason.** The lab invested first. In the infrastructure, the methodology, the flywheel that makes each project possible. Taking 15% immediately off every dollar of revenue, before any project distribution, means the lab's operations are never dependent on any single project's success or failure. If WAXFEED fails, the lab still runs. That independence is structural, not aspirational.

**Revenue participation rights, not equity, is the correct instrument for early believers.** Equity is permanent. Revenue participation rights are time-bounded. Year 1 from first disbursement, then rights expire. This means:
- Early believers are rewarded for backing work before it is obvious, which is the correct incentive
- The lab retains full ownership after year 1, with no dilution into future rounds
- External investors cannot accumulate positions that give them leverage over research decisions
- The team owns the methodology permanently

**The two-tier legal structure (gifts vs. Reg D) keeps the independence clean at different scales.** Under-$1,000 contributions are gifts with perks. No financial return, no SEC registration, no investor relationship to manage. This tier builds community and covers small project costs without creating any obligations. $1,000+ contributions are private revenue participation agreements, not publicly solicited. The relationship-based path, conversation before agreement, means only genuine believers who understand the model enter at this tier. No anonymous investor taking a position they can use as leverage.

**Time outranks money in the distribution structure.** Domain leaders receive the largest project pool share. Time collaborators outrank financial believers. This is not just philosophical. It is the structural protection against mission drift. If financial believers held more weight than the team doing the work, commercial pressures could start to distort what gets built and what gets published. The structure prevents that by design.

**IP transfers to the lab at project start, non-negotiably.** The methodology belongs to the lab regardless of what happens to any individual project. A project that fails does not take the instruments it helped develop with it. The compounding methodology is the lab's core asset and is protected from fragmentation.

## FUNDING SEQUENCING

1. **Immediate:** Grant applications to NIH (clinical AI), Knight Foundation (GPS/AVDP), Mozilla (independent research). Non-dilutive, builds credibility, runs in parallel with everything else.
2. **Near-term:** First IΔ proof contract closed. This is the lab's first commercial validation and the precondition for any equity conversation.
3. **Medium-term:** Approach 1517 and aligned early-stage investors after the first proof contract, with case study in hand, on the lab's terms.
4. **Project-level:** WAXFEED and GPS raise project-level capital independently once the proof contract model is established and their individual value propositions are validated.
5. **Long-term:** Standard licensing revenue reduces dependence on external capital and allows the lab to be increasingly selective about any further equity relationships.

The goal is to need external capital less over time, not more. Independence compounds.

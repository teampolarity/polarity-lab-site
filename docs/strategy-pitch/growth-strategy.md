## THE CORE DISTINCTION

Lab growth and product growth are different problems. Different timelines, different mechanics. They need separate strategies that reinforce each other, not compete. Conflate them and you make the wrong moves at the wrong moments.

**Lab growth** is about compounding intellectual authority. It moves slowly and durably.
**Product growth** is about demonstrating the return at scale. It moves in phases, one environment at a time.

---

## LAB GROWTH: COMPOUND THE AUTHORITY

The lab grows by being the institution that performed the trick first in each new environment. Every demonstration adds to a compounding loop:

Publish the methodology → institutional buyers run assessments → data deepens the instruments → stronger methodology attracts better buyers → repeat.

The citation trail is the growth mechanism. The more environments where the return has been proven, the harder it becomes for any competitor to catch up. Not because of proprietary technology, but because the cross-domain validity keeps expanding with every new domain.

The lab never needs to be the biggest. It needs to be the first and most credible. SFI didn't grow by scaling. It grew by naming the phenomenon before anyone else and never stopped being that institution.

**The independence growth loop — must stay intact:**

Proof contracts generate revenue → revenue funds research without dilutive capital → research validates instruments → validated instruments attract better proof contracts → better contracts generate more revenue → independent revenue means the lab chooses its investors rather than needing them → selectivity protects the methodology's trustworthiness → trustworthiness is the asset everything depends on.

The growth strategy that breaks this loop breaks the lab. The growth strategy that compounds it builds something that gets harder to compete with every year.

## PRODUCT GROWTH: SEQUENCING

Each product has a different growth mechanic. Trying to grow all four simultaneously without sufficient resources degrades the quality of each demonstration. Sequencing is the strategy.

---

### Phase 1: IΔ first — establish the standard

**Goal:** One published paper. One paying institutional buyer. One case study.

No consumer scale needed. The IΔ methodology is closest to validation and has the clearest institutional buyer waiting. Publish the paper. Get one clinical AI vendor or hospital system to run the first paid assessment. That single case study:

- Proves the proof contract model works
- Generates first institutional revenue
- Creates the first citation
- Positions the lab inside the regulatory conversation (FDA, DSA) before anyone asks
- Funds Phase 2

The regulatory tailwind creates demand pull. DSA auditors need a methodology. FDA reviewers need a measurement standard. Hospital systems need accountability infrastructure. This is a discovery problem, not a demand generation problem. The buyer is already looking. The paper makes the lab findable.

**Growth mechanic:** Citation compounds citation. Regulatory mandate adoption is the FICO moment. If one body requires the methodology, others follow.

---

### Phase 2: GPS — one city, density before expansion

**Goal:** Achieve density in Providence. Prove the geographic model. Build the template for every subsequent city.

Providence is already the home base. The creative community is present: RISD, Brown, local music scene, existing organizational relationships. GPS doesn't need to be national to be proven. It needs to work in one city, to reach the point where the community discovers what the platforms were hiding from them and tells each other.

**The geographic model:**
- City-by-city, never simultaneously
- Density before expansion — the platform fails without it
- Cultural adjacency over pure geography when expanding (Providence to Boston to Detroit to New Orleans, not Providence to Cincinnati)
- Local community organizations as the distribution seed, not social media

When Providence works, the pitch to the next city is a case study, not a promise. That case study is also the proof contract pitch to urban planners, city governments, and local media organizations who need the Proximity Index methodology.

**Growth mechanic:** Word of mouth within the city is the only mechanism that creates geographic density. The game mechanic draws people in. Community organizations distribute. The return — your city's culture becoming visible — spreads person to person.

---

### Phase 3: WAXFEED — consumer scale on the cultural moment

**Goal:** Seed round. Launch into a moment when the contrast between genuine taste and algorithmic prediction is already on people's minds.

Once IΔ has proven the institutional model and GPS has proven the geographic model, WAXFEED raises a seed round with actual evidence that the lab's methodology produces returns at multiple scales. The pitch to investors is no longer a promise. It's a pattern.

**The growth mechanic:** The cognitive profile as shareable artifact. The moment someone sees their actual taste mapped against what the algorithm had been feeding them is inherently social. They show people. The "this is who I actually am" realization is the viral unit. The cultural moment strategy accelerates it — don't launch into silence, enter when music discourse is already live and the frustration with algorithmic recommendation is already articulated.

**What not to do:** Compete on features with Spotify or Apple Music. The growth path is cognitive depth and authenticity of identity, not catalog size or interface quality.

---

### Phase 4: AVDP — parallel track, research-led growth

**Goal:** Prove format effectiveness with small N. Generate academic and media attention. Build the research case for the Format Effectiveness Measure.

AVDP runs in parallel with the other phases because YouTube distribution doesn't require a launch event and small N is sufficient to validate the format research. The research angle is the growth driver: academic citations, media publications, the attention research community that already suspects what engagement media does and wants rigorous proof.

**Growth mechanic:** Research attention, festival circuit, media publications. Consumer scale is not required to prove format effectiveness. The methodology can be validated with hundreds of viewers, not millions.

## THE COMPOUNDING DATASET

The dataset is the growth asset that compounds across all four products simultaneously and cannot be replicated by a late entrant.

- IΔ data deepens the methodology for AI honesty measurement
- GPS behavioral data feeds the Proximity Index validation
- WAXFEED's dataset feeds cognitive profiling that enriches IΔ's clinical deskilling research
- AVDP's format research adds another domain where the return will be measured (instrument design complete; validation study pending)

Every loop tightens every other loop. A competitor starting today would need to run all four platforms simultaneously for years to generate comparable data. And they couldn't do it independently — structural conflict of interest blocks every well-resourced institution that would otherwise have the capacity.

The data moat widens with every user, every assessment, every city. This is the growth asset that is genuinely non-replicable.

---

## WHAT GROWTH IS NOT

- Paid acquisition before product-market fit
- Platform dependency: building on any platform the lab would be measuring
- Scaling before the demonstration is proven in at least one environment per product
- Chasing consumer scale before institutional validation
- Growth for its own sake — the lab's gold is the methodology, not the user count

Growth that compromises the independence growth loop destroys more value than it creates.

---

## THE SUMMARY SEQUENCE

1. IΔ paper published and first proof contract closed — funds the lab, plants the flag
2. GPS density achieved in Providence — proves the geographic model
3. WAXFEED seed round raised on evidence, launched on cultural moment — proves consumer scale
4. AVDP research validated with small N, running in parallel throughout
5. Each proof enables the next raise, the next city, the next domain

The lab grows by proving the return one environment at a time until the methodology is the standard everything else is measured against.

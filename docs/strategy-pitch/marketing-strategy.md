## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

The demonstration is the marketing. Every time the return is staged in a new environment, the audience understands immediately what the lab is without needing it explained. You don't market the trick. You perform it. The performance is the marketing.

The lab doesn't need a large audience early. It needs the right audience to see the right demonstration at the right moment. One IΔ paper in the right journal, one clinical AI vendor who runs the assessment and publishes the results, one city where GPS achieves density and the community notices. Each of those is worth more than any campaign.

Build the demonstrations. The audience follows the proof.

---

## MARKETING THE LAB: OWN THE VOCABULARY

The most powerful marketing move available is naming the phenomenon so precisely that every conversation about it points back here. SFI did this with "complexity" and "emergence." Once those were SFI's terms, every conversation about complex adaptive systems cited SFI. The lab needs to do the same with the second threshold, counterproductivity measurement, and the return as a design standard.

Whoever names it owns the conversation. The lab names it first.

**The IΔ paper is the first marketing document that matters for the institutional channel.** Not a press release. Not a product launch. A published, peer-reviewed methodology that clinical AI vendors, DSA auditors, and FDA reviewers find when they search for what they already know they need. Whoever publishes first owns the citation trail. The paper is the flag planted.

**The website front door performs the pitch narrative.** Three movements. No explanation. The ordinary thing, the disappearance, the return. Visitors who feel it find the depth. Visitors who don't aren't the audience yet. See pitch-narrative.md for the copy.

## MARKETING THE FOUNDER: THE STORY IS THE ASSET

The founder ran a newsletter for a year to over 100 subscribers on productivity, tools, and wellbeing. That was the Pledge and Turn without the Prestige. Here are the tools, here's what they do and don't do, here's what I'm still looking for. Cosmos is the Prestige. The newsletter should resume now, with the full framework behind it, as the primary owned content channel.

The founder's story is the most powerful thing the lab has. ADHD, medicine, WBRU, going back for the original vision, Cosmos. Not as personal branding. As proof of concept. The founder is the first case study. Every piece of content that tells that story is also the pitch.

The story should be told repeatedly, in different forms, for different audiences. The shape stays the same: here is what the environments took, here is what the founder went back for, here is what was there waiting. Sankofa in every telling without naming it.

## MARKETING THE PRODUCTS

Every product follows the three movements regardless of channel or audience. Show the ordinary thing the user already uses. Surface what it's taking. Demonstrate the return. The shape is consistent. The execution is completely different.

**IΔ — institutional channel:**
- The research paper is the marketing
- Medical informatics conferences: AMIA, HIMSS
- Direct outreach to clinical AI vendors: Nuance, Epic, Google Health, Microsoft Health
- FDA comment periods on clinical AI oversight. Being in that conversation positions the lab as the measurement standard supplier before anyone asks.
- Hospital system innovation offices
- The buyer already exists and is already looking. This is a discovery problem, not a demand generation problem.

**WAXFEED — consumer channel:**
- Cultural moment strategy: enter when music discourse is already hot, not into the void
- The cognitive profile as shareable artifact. Something people want to see and show others.
- The hook: "What does your music taste reveal about how you actually think, versus what the algorithm has been training you to like"
- Music communities where the algorithmic frustration is already articulated (Reddit, Discord, niche forums)
- The newsletter seed audience is the starting point
- Do not compete on features with Spotify or Apple Music. Compete on cognitive depth and authenticity of identity.

**GPS — geographic channel:**
- Hyper-local before anything else. Neighborhood level, not city level.
- Local community organizations are the distribution channel, not social media
- The J'Juan Wilson connection and existing Providence network are the seed
- The game mechanic draws people in. The research is structural and invisible to the user.
- Word of mouth within the city is the only growth mechanism that works here. Paid acquisition cannot create geographic density.

**AVDP — research and media channel:**
- YouTube is both distribution and the thing being measured against
- The research angle is the marketing: what exact format conditions restore what engagement media removes
- Media publications, attention researchers, the audience that already knows something is wrong with what engagement media does to them
- Festival circuit for format validation research and visibility
- Small N is enough to generate academic and media attention

## CONTENT STRATEGY

**The newsletter:** Resume it under the lab's framework. The founder has the audience seed and the writing practice. The content is: here is what a designed environment is suppressing right now, here is what the counter-environment looks like, here is evidence the return is real. Published consistently. Not product updates. Intellectual frame.

**Thought leadership:** The founder writes about the second threshold, counterproductivity, the algorithm, the return. Not as a blogger. As the person building the methodology. The pieces should be rigorous enough to cite and readable enough to share.

**The research outputs:** Every paper, every validated instrument, every case study is a piece of marketing for the institutional channel. The research IS the content for that audience.

**What content is not:** Product announcements, feature updates, founder lifestyle content, generic startup advice. None of that serves the lab's positioning or builds the right audience.

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Paid acquisition before product-market fit in any channel
- Platform dependency: building primary presence on any platform the lab would be measuring
- Launching before the demonstration is ready. A half-performed trick is worse than no trick.
- Explaining the methodology when demonstrating it would work better
- Consumer press coverage too early: TechCrunch, Product Hunt. Wrong audience, wrong moment.
- Growing the audience before there is something worth following

## THE AUDIENCE THAT MATTERS FIRST

Not the biggest audience. The right one.

For the lab: researchers, regulators, institutional buyers who need the methodology.
For IΔ: clinical AI decision-makers, medical informatics community, FDA.
For WAXFEED: music-obsessed people who feel the algorithm doesn't understand them.
For GPS: the Providence creative community who already suspect what the platform is hiding from them.
For AVDP: people who already know engagement media is doing something to them and want proof.

Each of these audiences is small, specific, and reachable without scale. Start there. Let the proof expand the audience.

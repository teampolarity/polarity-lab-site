## THE CORE FRAME

The lab sells the return. The instrument is how you prove it happened. The standard is how it scales. Everything maps back to that one thing across every buyer, every environment, every product.

This is not conventional SaaS. It is not a research institution that lives on grants. It is not a consulting firm. The monetization model reflects what the lab actually is: the institution that performs the return and proves it was real. Revenue follows from the demonstration, not from the product.

---

## WHAT THE RETURN LOOKS LIKE AT DIFFERENT SCALES

**The consumer** buys the experience of it. Here is the taste you actually have, not the one the algorithm built for you. Here is your city's culture, not the one the platform decided you should see. Here is your executive function, intact, without the parts of you traded away to make it compliant. Personal. Direct. Felt before it's understood. The return at consumer scale is a product people inhabit. Cosmos, WAXFEED, GPS.

**The institution** buys the proof of it. A hospital system doesn't buy IΔ because they want a research paper. They buy it because they need to demonstrate that their clinical AI deployment is returning honest reasoning to the physician rather than overriding it. The instrument makes the return legible and auditable in a form that can be shown to boards, regulators, and accreditation bodies. The return at institutional scale is an assessment, a score, a validated methodology.

**The regulator** buys the standard for it. DSA auditors don't have a benchmark for what "giving something back to a community" even means in the context of discovery algorithms. The Proximity Index defines it. FDA reviewers don't have a measurement framework for what clinical AI honesty looks like at the output level. IΔ defines it. Once defined, it is enforceable. The return at regulatory scale is the standard itself. That's the supply the regulatory demand is actively looking for and currently cannot find.

**The investor** buys the methodology that generates it in every new environment. Not a product with a specific TAM. The portable capability, the algorithm applied on demand to whatever environment has crossed the second threshold. The return at investor scale is the compounding value of a methodology that works in every new domain it enters.

## THREE REVENUE CHANNELS, STACKED

The channels are designed to fund each other. Near-term institutional revenue funds the lab while consumer subscriptions build. Consumer subscriptions generate the data that validates the standard. The validated standard creates the licensing market. Each channel strengthens the others.

---

### Channel 1: Proof Contracts (B2B, near-term)

**What it is:** Institutions pay for the demonstration that their environment can return what it suppresses. The lab enters the environment, measures what was suppressed using the relevant instrument, designs or evaluates the counter-environment, and produces a validated before-and-after. That demonstration is the deliverable.

**Who buys it:**
- Clinical AI vendors who need to show their deployments maintain rather than erode clinical judgment (IΔ)
- Hospital systems facing FDA clinical AI oversight requirements (IΔ)
- Platforms subject to DSA annual audit requirements who need a Proximity Index score (Proximity Index)
- Media companies evaluating their format's effect on viewer cognitive restoration (Format Effectiveness Measure)
- Urban planners and city governments assessing discovery infrastructure (Proximity Index)

**Why the demand exists now:** DSA mandates annual independent audits of 23+ Very Large Online Platforms. Audit reports show massive divergence because measurement standards don't exist. The FDA is actively expanding oversight of clinical AI decision support. Both represent existing regulatory demand with no supply of measurement standards. CDT published a piece called "Auditing in the Dark" about exactly this gap. The buyer is already looking. The paper makes the lab findable.

**Pricing model:**
- Project-based assessment fees at the low end: $25k-$150k per engagement depending on scope and environment
- Annual monitoring contracts once the baseline assessment is complete: recurring revenue
- Methodology licensing at the high end once validated and published: institutional licenses for audit firms who want to use the instrument themselves

**Why this channel comes first:** Highest ticket, lowest volume, no consumer acquisition required. One validated IΔ deployment with one clinical AI vendor generates institutional revenue, creates the first paying case study, plants the citation trail, and funds the lab while the consumer products grow. This is where monetization begins.

---

### Channel 2: Return Subscriptions (Consumer, medium-term)

**What it is:** Individuals pay for ongoing access to the counter-environments the lab operates. Monthly recurring revenue that compounds with the dataset. Every subscriber deepens the instrument, which validates the methodology, which strengthens the proof contracts.

**The products:**
- **Cosmos:** Personal operating system for cognitive restoration without suppression of identity. Already running. The first proof of the return, on the founder. The model for everything else.
- **WAXFEED:** Music platform that measures the gap between algorithmic prediction and genuine taste, surfaces the authentic cognitive profile, and tests whether a differently designed recommendation environment closes the gap.
- **GPS (Polarity GPS):** Location-based discovery platform that measures the gap between what a community produces and what platforms surface to that community. Requires geographic density to function. One city at a time.

**Why this channel matters beyond revenue:** The subscription is not just revenue. It is the research running continuously. Every user interaction deepens the behavioral dataset that makes the instruments more precise, which strengthens the proof contracts, which funds the lab, which funds more product development. The consumer channel is the data engine. No institutional buyer can replicate the dataset it generates because they cannot operate the counter-environments that generate it.

**Pricing model:**
- Freemium entry on WAXFEED and GPS to achieve initial density
- Monthly subscription tiers: $8-20/month depending on product and feature depth
- Cosmos: premium subscription given the personal OS depth, $15-25/month

**Sequencing within this channel:**
- Cosmos is already running. Immediate.
- WAXFEED: needs sufficient behavioral data before the cognitive gap measure becomes meaningful. Launch when the dataset is deep enough to show genuine divergence between algorithmic prediction and actual response.
- GPS: needs geographic density before it works at all. Providence first, density before expansion, never simultaneous multi-city launch.

---

### Channel 3: Standard Licensing (Infrastructure, long-term)

**What it is:** The FICO layer. Once IΔ is published, validated, and established as the methodology, clinical AI vendors need it to prove their deployments are honest. Once the Proximity Index is the standard DSA auditors reach for, platforms need to run it to demonstrate compliance. The standard becomes the product. Whoever holds the standard licenses it.

**The historical parallel:** FICO became mandatory not by being the best researcher but by owning the number that mortgage lenders couldn't ignore. Freddie Mac mandated its use in 1995 and from that point the score organized the entire mortgage market. The regulatory tailwind, DSA and FDA clinical AI, is the equivalent forcing function. If one regulatory body cites the IΔ methodology as the measurement standard, every clinical AI deployment in that jurisdiction needs it.

**What this looks like in practice:**
- Audit firms licensing the Proximity Index methodology to conduct their own DSA audits
- Clinical AI vendors paying annual licensing fees to use the IΔ framework in their own product validation
- Medical schools incorporating IΔ into clinical AI curriculum. Institutional licenses.
- Urban planning consultancies using the Proximity Index framework for city-level assessments
- The methodology cited in regulatory guidance documents, creating mandatory adoption

**Why this channel is long-term:** It requires the methodology to be rigorous enough that other institutions choose to use it, and eventually for regulatory bodies to reference it in guidance. That requires the paper, the first proof contracts, the validated case studies, and the citation trail. All of which the earlier channels produce. This channel emerges from the validation that Channels 1 and 2 generate. It cannot be rushed.

**Pricing model:**
- Annual institutional licenses: $10k-$100k depending on institution size and usage
- Per-assessment licensing: fee per audit or deployment assessed using the methodology
- Certification program: institutions certified to administer the instruments. Training and ongoing certification fees.

## HOW THE LAB-AS-FUND STRUCTURE HOLDS THIS TOGETHER

Each project generates its own revenue, raises its own capital where needed, and contributes 15% back to the lab. This means:

- IΔ can attract clinical AI partnerships, NIH grants, and hospital system contracts independently
- WAXFEED can raise a seed round on the consumer product and its behavioral dataset
- GPS can raise on the urban discovery and smart city angle
- AVDP can attract media research grants and format licensing deals

The lab's 15% cut across all of them means no single project has to carry the whole weight of the lab's operations. Project failure does not destroy the lab. The methodology persists independent of any single application.

**The critical constraint:** The lab-as-fund structure only works if each project maintains the research-revenue balance. Projects serve the methodology as much as the methodology serves the projects. If any project's commercial incentives start to distort its instrument's findings, the trust that makes the methodology valuable is broken. The 15%/85% split and the lab's independence are structural protections against this.

## THE PRACTICAL SEQUENCING

1. IΔ paper published and first proof contract closed. This is where it starts. No consumer scale required. One institutional buyer, one validated case study, first revenue.
2. Consumer subscriptions build in parallel. Cosmos already running. WAXFEED and GPS follow the sequencing in growth-strategy.md.
3. Standard licensing emerges as the methodology accumulates validation from Channels 1 and 2.
4. Grant revenue runs as a parallel non-dilutive track throughout. See funding-strategy.md.

The proof contract funds the lab while subscriptions build. The subscriptions generate the data that validates the standard. The standard creates the licensing market. Stack in sequence. Don't attempt all three simultaneously before Channel 1 is established.

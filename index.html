The Research Question

Can we measure how your brain organizes knowledge — just from how you talk?

A doctor can learn about your lungs by listening to you breathe with a stethoscope. We're asking: can we learn about your brain's knowledge structure by analyzing how you talk — with algorithms?

The Framework

Conversational Connectomics

Five interlocking constructs that extract cognitive signals from natural language

Units of Cognition

12 TYPES

ASSERTION, ACTION, QUESTION, PLAN, EMOTION, EVALUATION, REQUEST, MEMORY_REF, REPAIR, SOCIAL, META, INTENTION

MEG-Proxy States

12 MODES

Cognitive modes inferred from language patterns, standing in for what brain imaging would show

Polarity Points

0-100 SCALE

Connection strength via Bayesian Beta-Bernoulli estimation. PP = f(R, C, T, Q)

BrainID

POL-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX

Unique cognitive fingerprint from state transitions, PP distribution, temporal patterns

Consolidation Gate

MEMORY STAGES

LABILE → STABILIZING → CONSOLIDATED → PERMANENT. Mimics hippocampal gating.

Reading Room

Exploring Conversational Connectomics - inferring how concepts connect in your mind using natural language. 258-page framework document. 12 computational experiments. 2 provisional patents.

PNAS 2010

Your Brain Syncs With Who You Talk To

Stephens, Silbert & Hasson show that during real conversation, brains literally synchronize. The better they sync, the better the understanding.
Neuron 2017

Your Brain Chunks Life Into Episodes

Baldassano et al. reveal that the brain automatically segments experience into discrete events. These boundaries trigger memory encoding.
Nature Reviews Neuroscience 2009

Your Mind Is a Network

Bullmore & Sporns establish that the brain is organized like a network with clusters, hubs, and efficient pathways. Thinking is the pattern across this network.

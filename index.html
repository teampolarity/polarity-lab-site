Who We Are

Technology is engineered to hold your attention. We build things designed to improve your life.

Most platforms make money when you keep scrolling. That incentive has produced a measurable result: people are more isolated, more anxious, and less able to focus than a generation ago. We study the mechanism, and we build the antidote.

Most of what we build falls into one of two categories.

The first is what we call a therapeutic environment: a place or experience designed so that going through it produces a real, specific change in you. Think about the last time you finished a film and immediately called someone you hadn't spoken to in months. The film didn't tell you to call. You just came out of it different. That's what we mean: the environment does the work. You leave less isolated, thinking more clearly, trusting yourself more. Not because you tried. Because of what it put you through.

The second is a diagnostic instrument: a tool that gives you a number for something that doesn't have one yet. A blood pressure cuff doesn't ask how you feel. It gives a reading that means the same thing in any clinic in the world. We build that for things medicine hasn't gotten to yet: how disconnected someone has become from real relationships, how much an AI is steering their thinking without them knowing it, how much a platform is quietly eroding someone's ability to make their own decisions. A number that can be studied, tracked, and acted on.

The Research

Technology has produced a new class of disease.

It is documented. It is accelerating.

AI-Psychosis

People losing grip on reality through sustained LLM interaction. An emerging clinical phenomenon documented in case reports and psychiatric literature, with patients presenting with delusional thinking and an inability to distinguish AI output from lived experience.

No clinical definition exists. No diagnostic criteria. No treatment protocol.

Loneliness Epidemic

The U.S. Surgeon General declared loneliness a public health epidemic in 2023. Measurable at population scale. Directly correlated with technology adoption curves: rising social media use, declining in-person time.

Documented. Quantified. No standard treatment pathway built for platform-induced cases.

Cognitive Atrophy

The brain discards unused skills. As AI handles cognitive work, human capacity atrophies. Clark and Chalmers showed cognition extends into tools. When the tool handles it, you lose it. Decades of supporting research across memory, navigation, and critical thinking.

The mechanism is understood. The measurement infrastructure to track it at an individual level doesn't exist yet.

Sycophancy Feedback Loop

AI systems are trained to agree with users. We adapt to trust them. The result is an echo chamber that degrades independent judgment at scale. People become progressively less capable of evaluating claims without AI reinforcement.

No corrective mechanism exists. No benchmark for how far any given person or system has drifted.

These are not separate problems. They are symptoms of one pathology: technology-induced isolation from other humans, from our own cognition, from accumulated human knowledge.

Traditional medicine operates on a 10 to 15 year cycle. The disease moves at the speed of a software update.

1

Name it precisely

Terms like "screen addiction" and "social media anxiety" are too vague to study or treat. We define specific mechanisms with enough clinical precision to name, replicate, and eventually diagnose: what is atrophying, what is being lost, and through exactly what process.

2

Build the diagnostic instrument Core research

This is the primary work. Before medicine could treat high cholesterol, it needed a test that produced a number. We build that infrastructure for harms that don't have numbers yet. Independent scoring tools any researcher, clinician, or regulator can run and reproduce. Integrity Delta is the first.

3

Build the antidote

Once you can measure the damage, you can design an environment that reverses it and verify it actually works. Therapeutic environments are the application layer: built on top of the diagnostic foundation, validated against it.

Primary Research · Active Diagnostic Instrument
Integrity Delta

There is no independent, standard way to measure how honest an AI is. The companies that build these systems define and measure their own safety metrics. No outside accountability. No reproducible benchmark.

Integrity Delta is that benchmark: a scoring instrument any researcher, enterprise, or regulator can run against any AI system to produce a consistent, auditable measurement of how much it steers users toward what they want to hear rather than what is actually true.

Resolution Valley: Core Hypothesis

Mid-tier models (7B to 13B parameters) occupy the most dangerous zone: smart enough to form correct answers internally, too weak to resist user pressure to agree. This is the parameter range hospitals and enterprises deploy today.

Diagnostic Instrument
Proximity Index
A scoring methodology developed in partnership with MUSEOFRI (Providence, RI, 2026). Two inputs: cultural output produced by or for a community, and what discovery platforms actually surface to that community. The gap between those two numbers is the score. Zero means everything made for you reaches you. A high score means your own culture is algorithmically invisible to you.
Finding: Across BIPOC-led communities in Providence, the gap was not caused by a lack of output. Communities were actively producing. The problem was in the pathways, not the product, giving PolarityGPS a specific, measurable target to fix.

Research Observation
Music Discovery Erosion
Streaming platforms algorithmically concentrate music discovery. The top 1% of tracks account for the overwhelming majority of streams across catalogs of hundreds of millions of songs. Recommendation systems replay what you already know. DJs and deep listeners had no platform built around their actual behavior.
Observation: Algorithmic distribution is compressing musical identity at scale. What people listen to is narrowing, not expanding. No instrument built yet. The observation was the design brief.

Research Observation
Conversational Depth Decline
Studies consistently show that the presence of a smartphone, even face-down and unused, measurably reduces the quality of in-person conversation. People are having shorter, shallower exchanges and fewer of the extended, unscripted conversations that build actual connection. Real conversation has become unusual enough that most people haven't had one in weeks.
Observation: Watching people genuinely talk to each other changes how you want to interact. Genuine conversation has become unusual enough that modeling it has therapeutic value. No instrument built yet.
→
In Production Therapeutic Environment
AVDP
A Very Distant Perspective. A documentary interview series of real, unscripted conversations about being human. Built on the finding that watching genuine conversation is itself therapeutic. It models something people have largely stopped doing and makes them want to do it themselves.
Proof of concept

The lab filmed Zay, a potter in the US, creating and describing his ceramic work. The episodes reached Mateo, a store owner in Canada. He saw Zay's work and wanted to carry it. The lab connected them directly. Two people across countries who would never have met, connected through the environment.

AVDP doesn't just create content. It creates connections.

Research Observation · Instrument in Design
Style Drift
Fashion recommendation systems are built around engagement and trending content, not individual identity. Sustained exposure homogenizes aesthetic expression. People's style gradually converges toward what platforms surface rather than what reflects them. The mechanism is invisible: it doesn't feel like influence, it feels like taste.
Observation: Fashion is increasingly a mirror of what platforms surface, not who people are. The instrument being designed will produce a score that makes this measurable for the first time.
→
In R&D Therapeutic Environment
Phresh
Personal styling and fashion discovery built on computer vision and identity modeling. Built to understand who you actually are: your body, your aesthetic history, your genuine taste. It surfaces clothing that fits all three. Not the feed's version of you.

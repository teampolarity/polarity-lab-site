What is CCX?

Conversational Connectomics

A new way to understand how your mind organizes ideas by analyzing how you talk. We map how concepts connect through natural conversation—turning dialogue into insight.

How it works

1

The Gap

Conversations are rich with meaning, but we rarely analyze them systematically. Valuable patterns go unnoticed.

2

The Insight

How you connect ideas in speech mirrors how you organize thoughts. Your dialogue is a window into cognition.

3

Our Approach

CCX maps these conversational patterns, revealing how your ideas connect and evolve over time.

The Science

How We Read Conversations

When you talk, your brain leaves clues. Here are the five things we look for.

What Type of Thought

12 TYPES

Are you asking, remembering, planning, or feeling? There are 12 basic types of thoughts. We notice which ones you use most.

Your Brain's Gear

12 MODES

Like a car has gears, your brain has modes. Planning mode, memory mode, creative mode. We can tell which gear you're in.

How Ideas Link

0-100 SCALE

When you hear "beach," do you think of family vacations or summer jobs? We measure how strongly any two ideas connect for you.

Your Thinking Style

UNIQUE TO YOU

Everyone thinks a little differently. Like a fingerprint, no two minds are exactly alike. We capture what makes yours unique.

How Memories Form

FRAGILE → SOLID

New memories are like wet cement. Over time, they harden. We track where your memories are in that process.

Safety & Ethics

Cognitive monitoring could enable surveillance and abuse. We are not naive about this. These principles are foundational.

User-Only Access

Your cognitive data is yours alone. No one else sees it.

No Default Sharing

Caregiver access requires explicit, revocable consent.

Coercion Detection

Patterns suggesting forced access trigger protective protocols.

Right to Deletion

Full data purge on request. No exceptions.

No Predictive Policing

Never used for risk scoring or preemptive judgment.

IRB Required

Clinical validation proceeds only with ethics board approval.

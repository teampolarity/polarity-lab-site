## What This Role Is

The lab exists because the founder built something he loved to build. That is not a footnote. Cosmos, the instruments, the thesis about the second threshold -- these came from someone doing their actual work, following genuine curiosity, building systems that reflected how they actually think. The theory is downstream of the practice. The delegation structure exists to protect the conditions that made the theory possible, not to replace them.

Every other role in the lab has a defined scope, a set of deliverables, and a boundary where it stops and something else begins. This one is different. The Executive Director holds the things that don't transfer -- the research agenda, the methodology, the thesis, the decision about which domains the lab enters and on what terms. Everything else is built to reduce the operational load on this role so that those things get full attention. When they don't, the lab drifts.

The founder role is a building and clarity role. The clarity work is keeping the vision precise enough that everyone operating under it knows what they're optimizing for and what they're not. The building work is the continued development of the theoretical framework, the instruments, and the counter-environments that the lab is studying. Both are required. A founder who only manages clarity eventually loses the contact with the work that made the clarity possible in the first place.

One thing this lab should be practicing on itself: the roles are designed to minimize compliance maintenance and maximize authentic work. Nobody here should be spending most of their time on system upkeep rather than on what they're actually here to do. If a role ends up structured that way, the design is wrong.

## What Only This Role Does

**Builds the core systems.** The counter-environments -- Cosmos, GPS, WAXFEED -- are not just products. They are the founder's research practice made deployable. Continued development of the theoretical and technical architecture of these systems is founder work. Not because no one else can write code, but because the building is how the thinking develops. Domain leads and technical staff contribute execution. The founder contributes conception and the judgment about when the system is honest.

**Sets the research agenda.** Which environments the lab studies, which instruments get built, which questions are worth asking -- these are not committee decisions. The research agenda is the lab's core asset. Its integrity depends on the founder holding it.

**Decides which domains the lab enters.** New projects come from the founder identifying a new instance of the second threshold that is measurable and for which a counter-environment can be designed. No collaborator proposes a new domain and launches it. That conversation starts with the founder and either results in a new research program or it doesn't.

**Defines instrument methodology.** The lab's instruments are its scientific contribution. IΔ is defined and piloted. The others (working names: CGM, SAM, FEM, CRM, Proximity Index) are in development and their names, scoring behavior, and definitions are not yet confirmed. Their methodological integrity — what they measure, why they measure it that way, and what would falsify them — is the founder's responsibility. Domain leads run the instruments. The founder is accountable for whether they remain honest.

**Makes all weight adjustment decisions.** Collaborator weight upgrades, reductions, and exits are the founder's call. The Lab Manager surfaces the performance picture. The domain lead gives context. The founder decides. These decisions are not delegated.

**Holds the single-member LLC.** The LLC structure is not a formality. It is the governance architecture that protects the methodology from external capture. The founder is the sole member. That does not change based on contribution, tenure, or relationship. If someone believes it should change, that conversation happens explicitly, not implicitly.

**Signs off on institutional commitments.** Any agreement that binds the lab — institutional partnerships, grant commitments, licensing agreements, scope of work contracts — goes through the founder before execution. Legal drafts, Legal Manager tracks status, domain leads negotiate scope, but the founder signs. The founder's role here is approval, not negotiation.

## What This Role Delegates

**Day-to-day operations:** The Lab Manager runs quarterly reviews, tracks agreements, manages onboarding, and monitors disbursement readiness. The Lab Manager is the operational layer. The founder gets the summary and the flags, not the underlying task management.

**Technical architecture:** The Technical Director holds the technical reality. When the lab makes technical commitments to external parties, those commitments have Technical Director input. The founder does not need to hold the technical details.

**Legal mechanics:** Legal drafts agreements and handles counsel coordination. The founder reviews and signs. The founder does not do legal drafting.

**Domain-level research execution:** Domain leads run their projects. They make the day-to-day research decisions, manage their collaborators, and advance their instruments. The founder is the backstop on methodology integrity and the escalation point for decisions that affect the lab's broader research posture.

**Initiation of institutional conversations:** The Director of Strategy runs first meetings with potential partners, funders, and institutional contacts. Their job is to represent the lab's work, qualify the opportunity, and brief the founder before the founder engages. The founder does not cold-reach. The founder enters a conversation when there is already something substantive to close or advance.

**Business development and financial believer relationships.** The Director of Strategy owns the full arc of these relationships: identification, warm-up, first meetings, qualification, and the financial believer conversations. The Director operates within the boundaries defined in the legal brief. The founder defines the terms of what the lab offers and approves who gets offered it. The founder is not in those conversations unless something unusual warrants it.

**Grant application execution.** Grant writing is a collaboration between the Director of Strategy, domain leads, and the Technical Director. The founder sets the scientific framing, reviews drafts, and approves submission. The founder does not draft from scratch or coordinate the application process.

## What Requires Founder Attention Right Now

The distinction that matters here: some items require the founder to run the full conversation. Others require the founder to make an introduction or set direction, then hand off until there is something substantive to close. The two are not the same, and conflating them is how the founder becomes a bottleneck on things that don't require bottlenecking.

**Academic partnership outreach.** The Director of Strategy identifies specific faculty contacts at Brown Psychiatry and MIT, sends the introduction email, and runs the first 30-minute meeting. The founder's job is to brief Strategy on what the IΔ pilot data shows, what the lab needs from a co-PI, and who the right scientific conversation partner would be. The founder re-engages when there is a faculty member who is genuinely interested in co-authorship and co-PI credit. That is a founder conversation. The cold introduction is not.

**First IΔ proof contract.** The Director of Strategy identifies the right targets (clinical AI vendors, hospital systems) and runs qualification conversations. The founder's job is to define what "qualified" means: what problem they need to have, what scale of deployment matters, what a real buyer looks like versus a curious one. When Strategy brings a qualified prospect, the founder takes the meeting and closes. The founder is not running the outreach.

**Patent counsel engagement for 63/940,728.** The deadline for the cognitive biometric authentication provisional is December 2026. Legal coordinates with counsel. The founder's job is to write a 2-3 page technical briefing describing the invention and the strategic context -- what problem it solves, why it matters, what prior art to be aware of. That document should exist within 60 days. Legal then drives the process.

**Defensive publication for 63/960,633.** The Conversational Connectomics provisional should be defensively published rather than converted. The founder writes the technical disclosure document -- this cannot be delegated because it requires describing what was actually conceived and filed. Target: within 60 days. Legal coordinates the submission to Research Disclosure.

**Knight Foundation LOI.** The GPS / Proximity Index grant outline is ready. The Director of Strategy drafts the LOI from the existing outline. The founder reviews and approves the scientific framing before submission. Target: within 60 days.

**Financial believer conversations.** The Director of Strategy owns these conversations. The founder's job is to decide which projects open a financial believer pool and on what terms, and to approve who gets offered access. Strategy manages the relationships, has the conversations, and handles the ask. The founder is available if a specific conversation warrants their direct involvement -- that is the exception, not the default.

## How This Role Gets Paid

The Executive Director is part of the core pool — the lab's 15% share of revenue distributed across lab-level roles using a points system that resets each quarter. The core pool points and activity bonuses for this role are documented in polarity-fund-model.md.

The honest version: the lab doesn't pay the founder a market salary. Not yet. The structure is designed so that when the lab's projects hit their runway thresholds and disbursements begin, the distribution flows to everyone who's been contributing — including the founder through the core pool. Until then, the work runs on the conviction that the thesis is real and the proof is coming.

The LLC structure means the founder also participates as the sole member in any residual value the entity holds. That is a different channel than the core pool and not the primary compensation mechanism while the lab is pre-disbursement.

## The Thing That Doesn't Transfer

The lab's methodology is the thesis: that designed environments have a second threshold, that suppression is measurable, and that recovery is real and provable. The instruments are only defensible as scientific instruments because the person holding the methodology is independent. The moment external governance can redirect the research agenda, the instruments become products and the science becomes marketing.

This is why the LLC is single-member. This is why financial believers get revenue participation rights, not equity. This is why every collaborator agreement specifies that they have no governance rights. The structure is not about control. It is about keeping the methodology honest while the proof is still being built.

The founder holds this. Not because it's convenient or because it preserves power, but because it's what the lab was built to do. When the proof is complete enough to propagate on its own terms, the question of what governance looks like can be asked for real.
